The Brazilian patent system offers three main mechanisms for third parties to challenge patent applications or granted patents: **subsidy to technical examination** (Art. 31 of the LPI), during the examination phase; **administrative nullity** (Arts. 50-55), within the first six months after grant; and **judicial nullity** (Arts. 56-57), at any time during the patent's term. Each has distinct deadlines, costs, grounds, and effects.

This guide consolidates the three mechanisms, their legal requirements, the steps to file each one, and strategies for choosing the most suitable route.

If you are not yet familiar with the patent filing process, first see our [complete guide to patent filing at the INPI]({{< relref "posts/guia-deposito-patente-inpi-2026/" >}}).

## Overview of the Challenge Mechanisms

| Mechanism | Legal Basis | When to Use | Deadline | Who Can File | Cost |
|-----------|------------|-------------|----------|--------------|------|
| **Subsidy to Examination** | Art. 31 LPI | During the technical examination of the application | From publication until the end of examination | Any interested party | Free (no specific fee) |
| **Administrative Nullity** | Arts. 50-55 LPI | After grant | Up to 6 months from grant | Any person with legitimate interest | Petition fee (code 272) |
| **Judicial Nullity** | Arts. 56-57 LPI | At any time | Throughout the patent's term | INPI or any person with legitimate interest | Court costs + attorneys' fees |

## 1. Subsidy to Technical Examination (Art. 31 of the LPI)

The subsidy to examination is the simplest and most accessible mechanism for third parties to influence the examination of a patent application. Any person may submit documents and information that assist the INPI examiner in forming their technical opinion.

### Legal Basis

> **Art. 31.** Once the patent application is published and until the end of the examination, interested parties may submit documents and information to support the examination.
>
> **Sole paragraph.** The examination shall not begin before 60 (sixty) days have elapsed from the publication of the application.

### Main Features

- **Who can submit:** any interested party — neither legitimate interest nor being a party to the proceeding is required
- **When:** from the publication of the application in the RPI (approximately 18 months after filing) until the final decision on the technical examination
- **What to submit:** prior art documents (patents, scientific articles, technical publications), technical arguments, expert reports
- **Cost:** there is no specific fee for submitting subsidies (service included in the "Other petitions" category or at no direct cost)
- **Effect:** the examiner considers the submitted material, but the subsidy author does not become a party to the proceeding — there is no right to appeal the decision

### What to Include in a Subsidy to Examination

A well-prepared subsidy to examination should contain:

1. **Application identification**: process number BR 10 20XX XXXXXX-X
2. **Prior art documents**: copies of patents, articles, or publications showing that the invention is not novel or does not involve an inventive step
3. **Comparative table**: mapping between the application claims and the prior art documents, highlighting each disclosed technical feature
4. **Technical reasoning**: explanation of why the prior art document anticipates or renders obvious the claimed invention
5. **Translations**: if the prior art document is in a foreign language, it is recommended to provide a simple translation of the most relevant excerpts

### How to File

The subsidy is filed through a petition in the INPI's petitioning system (service 200 or directly via the Patent Services Module, depending on the available version). See our [guide on how to check the status of processes at the INPI]({{< relref "posts/como-consultar-andamento-processo-inpi/" >}}) to understand the petitioning interface.

> ⚠️ **Critical deadline:** the sole paragraph of Art. 31 prevents the examination from starting before 60 days from publication. This means that, right after publication in the RPI, there is a 60-day window during which the examiner has not yet begun the analysis — the best time to submit relevant subsidies.

## 2. Administrative Nullity (Arts. 50 to 55 of the LPI)

Administrative nullity is the mechanism to challenge a granted patent directly before the INPI, without resorting to the Judiciary.

### Legal Basis

> **Art. 50.** The nullity of a patent shall be declared administratively when:
>
> I - any of the legal requirements have not been met;
>
> II - the report and claims do not comply with the provisions of arts. 24 and 25, respectively;
>
> III - the object of the patent extends beyond the content of the originally filed application; or
>
> IV - during processing, any essential formalities indispensable for grant have been omitted.
>
> **Art. 51.** The nullity proceeding may be initiated ex officio or upon request of any person with legitimate interest, within 6 (six) months counted from the grant of the patent.

### Procedure Deadlines

| Step | Deadline |
|------|----------|
| Nullity request | Up to 6 months from grant |
| Patent holder's response | 60 days from notification |
| INPI technical opinion | After holder's response |
| Parties' comments on the opinion | 60 common days |
| Decision of the INPI President | After comments |

### Who Has Legitimate Interest

The INPI does not require strict proof of legitimate interest at the initiation stage, but the consolidated administrative jurisprudence (CGREC Decision Collections) considers that:

- Direct competitors in the same market have presumed legitimate interest
- Industry associations have an interest in defending collective interests
- The INPI may initiate ex officio at any time (Art. 51, caput)

### Costs (INPI 2026 Table)

Values vary according to the applicant's size. Individuals, MEI, ME, EPP, ICTs, and non-profit entities are entitled to a 50% discount. The full official table is available on the [INPI website](https://www.gov.br/inpi/pt-br/inpi-data/precificacao-dos-servicos/NovaTabeladeRetribuiesINPI_PortariaINPI_Final_20_dez_25.pdf).

| Service | Code | Full Price | With 50% Discount |
|---------|------|------------|-------------------|
| Administrative nullity of patent | 272 | ~R$ 800 | ~R$ 400 |
| Holder's response to nullity | 280 | ~R$ 800 | ~R$ 400 |

> Exact amounts depend on the updated fee table. Always check the [INPI costs page](https://www.gov.br/inpi/pt-br/servicos/custos-e-pagamento) for current values.

### Effects

> **Art. 48.** The nullity of a patent shall produce effects from the date of filing of the application.

This means that, if nullity is granted, the patent is considered never to have existed — the effects retroact to the filing date. It is the most severe consequence, superior to a simple revocation.

## 3. Judicial Nullity (Arts. 56 and 57 of the LPI)

The nullity action is the judicial mechanism to challenge a patent, with broader deadlines and greater legal certainty.

### Legal Basis

> **Art. 56.** The nullity action may be filed at any time during the patent's term, by the INPI or by any person with legitimate interest.
>
> **§ 1º** The nullity of the patent may be argued, at any time, as a matter of defense.
>
> **§ 2º** The judge may, preventively or incidentally, order the suspension of the patent's effects, subject to the applicable procedural requirements.
>
> **Art. 57.** The patent nullity action shall be filed in the venue of the Federal Court and the INPI, when not the plaintiff, shall intervene in the proceeding.

### Features

- **Deadline:** any time during the patent's term (unlike the administrative route, which has a 6-month deadline)
- **Venue:** Federal Court (Federal Judicial Section of the Federal District or of the plaintiff's/defendant's domicile)
- **Parties:** plaintiff (any person with legitimate interest) × defendant (patent holder), with mandatory intervention of the INPI
- **Defense:** nullity may be argued as a defense in any judicial action — even after the administrative route deadline has expired
- **Effect:** final and unappealable judgment → INPI publishes an annotation in the RPI for third-party knowledge

### Administrative Nullity vs. Judicial Nullity

| Aspect | Administrative Nullity | Judicial Nullity |
|--------|------------------------|------------------|
| Deadline | 6 months from grant | Any time (during term) |
| Cost | INPI fees (R$ 400-800) | Court costs + attorneys' fees |
| Complexity | Low to medium | High |
| Suspensive effect | No | Yes (injunctive relief) |
| Final instance | INPI President (administrative) | Federal Regional Court/Superior Court of Justice (judicial) |
| Appealable | Yes (new judicial nullity) | Yes (appeal, special appeal, extraordinary appeal) |

## 4. Appeal Against INPI Decision

If the INPI denies your patent application (or if you are the third party whose nullity request was rejected), an appeal may be filed with the INPI President.

### Deadlines and Procedure

- **Appeal deadline:** 60 days from the publication of the decision in the RPI
- **Reply:** 60 days for the opposing party to respond
- **Instance:** General Coordination of Appeals and Administrative Nullity Proceedings (CGREC) — subordinate to the INPI Presidency
- **Final decision:** rendered by the INPI President, closing the administrative instance

The CGREC publishes periodic compilations of consolidated jurisprudence. The Collections of 2nd Administrative Instance Decisions in Patents (2017 to 2023 editions) are available on the [INPI Appeals and Nullities page](https://www.gov.br/inpi/pt-br/servicos/recursos-e-nulidades) and are essential references for understanding the body's consolidated understanding.

## 5. Comparative Table: Which Route to Choose?

| Scenario | Recommended Route | Reason |
|----------|------------------|--------|
| Application still under examination | Subsidy to Examination (Art. 31) | Faster, free, no need for legitimate interest |
| Patent granted less than 6 months ago | Administrative Nullity | Cheaper and faster than the judicial route |
| Patent granted more than 6 months ago | Judicial Nullity | Administrative route deadline has expired |
| Urgency (risk of imminent harm) | Judicial Nullity + Injunction | Possibility of precautionary suspension of effects |
| Defense in an infringement action | Nullity Argument as Defense | Art. 56, §1º — can be argued at any time |
| Multiple patents by the same holder | Combine Subsidy + Administrative Nullity | Subsidies on pending applications + nullity on granted ones |

## 6. Practical Strategies

### For the Applicant (How to Defend Yourself)

- **Monitor the RPI weekly** after the publication of your application — any third party may submit subsidies
- **Respond to subsidies courteously** — the examiner considers the material, but you may present counter-arguments during examination
- **Keep the application always in order** (annuities paid, responses to office actions on time) — poorly maintained patents are easy targets for nullity
- **Consider prioritized processing** to accelerate grant — see our [complete guide to prioritized processing]({{< relref "posts/guia-tramite-prioritario-patentes-inpi-2026/" >}})

### For the Third Party (How to Challenge)

- **Conduct a thorough prior art search** — our [practical guide to patent searching at the INPI]({{< relref "posts/busca-patentes-inpi-guia-pratico/" >}}) shows how to find relevant documents
- **Document everything** — each assertion must be accompanied by documentary evidence
- **Prefer the subsidy to examination** if the application is still under analysis — it is free and does not require legitimate interest
- **Act quickly on administrative nullity** — the 6-month deadline from grant is short and non-extendable

## 7. Frequently Asked Questions

### Does the subsidy to examination suspend the processing of the application?

No. The examiner analyzes the submitted material together with the examination, but the proceeding is not suspended. The only restriction is that the examination cannot begin before 60 days from publication (Art. 31, sole paragraph).

### Can I submit subsidies anonymously?

Yes. Art. 31 mentions "interested parties" without requiring full identification. However, unidentified subsidies may carry less weight because the examiner cannot request clarifications or additional documents.

### Does administrative nullity prevent a judicial action?

No. The administrative decision of the INPI President can be challenged in court. Moreover, the judicial nullity action may be filed independently — even if an administrative decision has already been rendered.

### How much does a judicial nullity action cost?

Costs vary significantly: attorneys' fees, court costs (proportional to the value of the claim), technical expert reports (when necessary). For complex proceedings, the total cost may range from R$ 30,000 to R$ 150,000 or more. Consult a specialized IP attorney for accurate estimates.
