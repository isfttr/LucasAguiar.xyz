The National Bank for Economic and Social Development (BNDES) and the Funding Authority for Studies and Projects (Finep) announced on June 22, 2026, the availability of an additional R$ 140 billion to finance projects for the New Brazil Industry (NIB) until December 2026. With the new contribution, the total volume of resources mobilized by the industrial policy reaches over R$ 750 billion in the 2023-2026 period.

The announcement was made during BNDES's 74th-anniversary celebration ceremony in Rio de Janeiro, with the presence of President Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva and Vice President and Minister of Development, Industry, Trade, and Services, Geraldo Alckmin. Of the total amount, R$ 102.5 billion will be made available by BNDES and R$ 37.5 billion by Finep.

The most significant novelty, however, is the inclusion of artificial intelligence (AI) among the strategic sectors of industrial policy — alongside fertilizers, agricultural machinery, active pharmaceutical ingredients (APIs), biopharmaceuticals, advanced therapies, sustainable mobility, audiovisual, critical minerals, and dual-use technologies.

This article reconstructs the trajectory of over two decades of industrial policy in Brazil to show that this decision was not made overnight.

## The Long Road to New Brazil Industry

### 2004 — PITCE: The First Industrial Policy of the 21st Century

The Industrial, Technological, and Foreign Trade Policy (PITCE) was Brazil's first systemic attempt to articulate an industrial development agenda focused on innovation. Created by Law No. 11.080/2004, PITCE established the National Council for Industrial Development (CNDI) as a coordination body between the government and the productive sector ([CNDI no site do MDIC](https://www.gov.br/mdic/pt-br/composicao/se/cndi)).

### 2008 — PDP: Scale and Priority Sectors

The Productive Development Policy (PDP) succeeded PITCE with more ambitious goals and a sectoral focus: pharmaceuticals, defense, ICT, and renewable energies. It was the first Brazilian industrial policy to explicitly include information technologies as a strategic vector ([MDIC — Histórico da Política Industrial](https://www.gov.br/mdic/pt-br/assuntos/politica-industrial)).

### 2011 — Brasil Maior Plan

Launched with the motto "Innovate to Compete. Compete to Grow," the Brasil Maior Plan (PBM) brought payroll tax relief, tax incentives for innovation, and ambitious goals — to raise fixed capital investments from 18.4% to 23% of GDP ([Wikipédia — Plano Brasil Maior](https://pt.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plano_Brasil_Maior)).

### 2016–2022 — The Institutional Void

The CNDI was deactivated during the Temer government and not reactivated during the Bolsonaro government. For six years, Brazil operated without a coordinated industrial policy. The manufacturing industry's share of GDP persistently shrank ([Relatório de Transição 2022](https://static.poder360.com.br/2022/12/Relatorio-final-da-transicao-de-Lula.pdf)).

### April 2023 — Reestablishment of CNDI

Decree No. 11.482 reestablished the CNDI with the mission of proposing guidelines for the new industrial policy. Chaired by Vice President Geraldo Alckmin, the council brought together 21 ministries, representatives from industry, academia, and workers.

### January 2024 — Launch of New Brazil Industry

On January 22, 2024, the government launched NIB with R$ 300 billion in projected financing until 2026 and six missions: (1) sustainable agro-industrial chains, (2) health complex, (3) infrastructure and mobility, (4) digital transformation, (5) bioeconomy, and (6) defense ([Agência Brasil](https://agenciabrasil.ebc.com.br/economia/noticia/2024-01/entenda-o-programa-nova-industria-brasil), [G1](https://g1.globo.com/politica/noticia/2024/01/22/nova-politica-industrial-tera-r-300-bilhoes-em-financiamentos-ate-2026-anuncia-governo.ghtml)).

## The Expansion of NIB (2024–2026)

Between the launch and the June 2026 announcement, NIB underwent several expansions:

- **December 2023 (before launch):** MOVER Program — R$ 19.3 billion in tax incentives for automotive decarbonization ([Planalto](https://www.gov.br/planalto/pt-br/acompanhe-o-planalto/noticias/2023/12/mover-novo-programa-amplia-acoes-para-mobilidade-verde-e-descarbonizacao))
- **2024:** Private announcements of R$ 30.8 billion (GM R$ 7 bi, VW R$ 16 bi, BYD R$ 3 bi)
- **September 2024:** Expansion to defense and aerospace
- **December 2024:** Inclusion of strategic mineral chains
- **December 2025:** Embrapii receives R$ 255 million from MCTI ([Embrapii](https://embrapii.org.br/embrapii-e-mcti-anunciam-aporte-de-r-255-milhoes-para-novos-projetos-de-inovacao-e-expansao-da-rede-de-unidades/))

## The June 22, 2026 Announcement

### R$ 140 Billion Additional

The new contribution of R$ 140 billion raises NIB's total to R$ 750 billion (2023–2026). The composition is:

| Component | Value |
|------------|-------|
| Initial announcement (Jan/2024) | R$ 300 billion |
| Additional BNDES (Jun/2026) | R$ 106 billion |
| Additional Finep (Jun/2026) | R$ 34 billion |
| Embrapii (Jun/2026) | R$ 1 billion |
| MOVER Program | R$ 19.3 billion |
| **Total** | **~R$ 750 billion** |

### Artificial Intelligence as a Strategic Sector

The inclusion of AI among NIB's priority sectors is a milestone. Until now, AI appeared in industrial policy in a transversal way — within Mission 4 (digital transformation) — but not as a sector with dedicated financing lines. Now, AI companies can access BNDES and Finep credit with the same favorable conditions as sectors like health and defense ([Finep](https://www.finep.gov.br/w/nova-industria-brasil-tera-r-140-bilhoes-ate-dezembro-de-2026)).

According to Aloizio Mercadante, president of BNDES, the new investments "strengthen areas considered strategic for national development, such as artificial intelligence, biopharmaceuticals, and critical minerals" ([BNDES](https://www.bndes.gov.br/wps/portal/site/home/imprensa/noticias/conteudo/bndes-e-finep-anunciam-r-140-bilhoes-adicionais-para-nib)).

### Investe Indústria Brasil Portal

The Brazilian Agency for Industrial Development (ABDI) launched, during the event, the Investe Indústria Brasil portal — a tool to monitor projects and support companies interested in investing in the segments covered by NIB. The platform will serve as a point of articulation between companies and the government, identifying bottlenecks that hinder the implementation of new projects ([Tele.Síntese](https://telesintese.com.br/nova-industria-brasil-recebera-mais-r-140-bilhoes-ate-o-fim-de-2026-ia-esta-entre-os-setores-prioritarios/)).

### Embrapii: Record Contribution

Another relevant announcement was the R$ 400 million contribution to Embrapii, with a total counterpart of R$ 1 billion (R$ 200 million from BNDES, R$ 200 million from Finep, and R$ 600 million in partnerships). The resources will be allocated to structuring research, development, and innovation projects between companies and accredited Embrapii units ([Embrapii](https://embrapii.org.br/aporte-recorde-do-mcti-reforca-orcamento-de-r-1-bilhao-da-embrapii-para-inovacao-em-2026/)).

## What it Means to Place AI in Industrial Policy

The decision to include AI as a priority sector has concrete implications:

1.  **Dedicated Financing:** Unlike transversal policies (which depend on thematic calls for proposals), AI now has its own credit lines. Brazilian AI companies can access BNDES resources with favorable rates and terms.

2.  **Signal to the Market:** The government is saying that AI is not just information technology — it is productive infrastructure comparable to energy, logistics, and health. This attracts private investment.

3.  **Global Competition:** The United States (CHIPS Act, US$ 280 billion), the European Union (European Chips Act, € 43 billion), and China (Made in China 2025) already treat digital technology as industrial policy. Brazil enters this game late, but with a volume of resources that is unprecedented in recent history.

## Challenges

The R$ 750 billion are impressive numbers, but the history of industrial policies in Brazil shows that the bottleneck was never formulation — it was execution.

The Investe Indústria Brasil portal, launched by ABDI, is an explicit attempt to solve this problem, functioning as a bottleneck monitoring system. The question remains whether the public machine has the capillarity and agility to transform credit into factories, jobs, and technology by 2026.

BNDES estimates that the multiplier for NIB financing is between 2x and 3x — which would mean up to R$ 2.25 trillion in induced investments. If execution matches rhetoric, this could be the largest industrial investment cycle since PAC.
