On July 27, 2026, the INPI made available the test platform of the **Patent Services Module**, a new interface that promises to transform how applicants, attorneys, and holders track their patent processes. Although filing itself is still done through the legacy **e-Patentes** system, the new module already offers a personalized workspace, automatic notifications, and access sharing — a concrete step in the [automation of INPI services]({{< relref "posts/inpi-automation-roadmap-2025-2029/" >}}).

But how does patent filing actually work in Brazil in 2026? This guide covers the entire process, from initial registration to publication of the application, including the new features brought by the new module.

## What is a patent and what does it protect?

Before filing, it is essential to understand what you are applying for. A patent is a temporary property title granted by the State to the inventor in exchange for full disclosure of the invention. In Brazil, the [Industrial Property Law (Law No. 9.279/96)](https://www.planalto.gov.br/ccivil_03/leis/l9279.htm) — the LPI — governs the grant, maintenance, and extinction of patents.

There are two main types:

| Type | Protection | Duration | Example |
|------|----------|---------|---------|
| **Invention Patent (PI)** | New product or process that is inventive and has industrial application | 20 years from filing date | New chemical compound, production method |
| **Utility Model (MU)** | Functional improvement in the use or manufacture of a known object | 15 years from filing date | New tool shape that improves ergonomics |

For a detailed comparison between the two types, see our guide on [types of intellectual protection in Brazil]({{< relref "posts/tipos-protecao-intelectual-brasil-guia-completo/" >}}).

## Prerequisites: what to have on hand before starting

Before opening the system, gather these documents and information:

1. **Registration in PAG (INPI Registration System)** — mandatory to access any INPI system. If you don't have one yet, see the [guide to CNPJ and gov.br account access]({{< relref "posts/inpi-acesso-cnpj-conta-govbr-guia-2026/" >}}).
2. **Technical documentation of the invention**: descriptive report, claims, abstract, drawings (if applicable)
3. **Payment receipt for the GRU** (Federal Collection Guide) — initial filing fee
4. **Power of attorney** (if filing is done by an attorney)
5. **Priority document** (if claiming priority from a previous filing — e.g., PCT application or foreign filing via the Paris Convention)

> ⚠️ **Attention:** Since 2025, the INPI requires that legal entity applicants (CNPJ) and their attorneys have a **gov.br account** at silver or gold level to access the systems. Individuals (CPF) still use PAG login and password.

## Step by step: how to file a patent with the INPI

### Step 1: Prior art search

Before filing, it is highly recommended to conduct a prior art search to check if your invention is already known. The INPI itself offers search tools — see our [practical guide to patent searches at the INPI]({{< relref "posts/busca-patentes-inpi-guia-pratico/" >}}).

The search avoids wasting fees and helps draft more precise claims. The INPI accepts applications even without a prior search, but the rejection rate for lack of novelty is high.

### Step 2: Access e-Patentes

Patent application filing is done through the **e-Patentes** system, available at:

[https://www.gov.br/inpi/pt-br/servicos/patentes/deposito-de-patentes](https://www.gov.br/inpi/pt-br/servicos/patentes/deposito-de-patentes)

Access with your PAG login (CPF + password) or gov.br account (for CNPJ and attorneys). The system guides you through filling out the electronic forms.

### Step 3: Fill out the filing form

The electronic form requests:

- **Applicant data**: name, CPF/CNPJ, address
- **Inventor data**: name and qualification of the inventor(s)
- **Title of the invention**: clear and descriptive, in Portuguese
- **Type of application**: Invention Patent (PI) or Utility Model (MU)
- **IPC classification**: you can enter the international classification or leave it for the INPI to classify — see the [IPC classification guide]({{< relref "posts/classificacao-internacional-patentes-ipc-guia-brasil/" >}})
- **Priority**: if any, provide the country, number, and date of the previous filing

### Step 4: Attach the technical documents

The system accepts files in PDF, ODT, DOCX, and TXT formats. The mandatory documents are:

| Document | Description | Format |
|-----------|-----------|---------|
| Descriptive report | Complete and clear description of the invention | PDF/ODT |
| Claims | Defines the scope of protection | PDF/ODT |
| Abstract | Summary of the technical content (up to 200 words) | PDF/ODT |
| Drawings (if any) | Figures, flowcharts, diagrams | PDF/TIF |
| Sequence listing (biotech) | For biotechnological inventions | TXT |

Important tip: the **claims** are the most critical part of the application — they define what you want to protect. Draft them carefully, preferably with the help of a qualified industrial property agent. See [how prior art search accreditation works]({{< relref "posts/inpi-credenciamento-busca-anterioridade-patentes/" >}}).

### Step 5: Generate and pay the GRU

After filling out the data and attaching the documents, the system generates the **GRU (Federal Collection Guide)** for payment of the filing fee. The values in 2026 are:

| Applicant type | Invention Patent | Utility Model |
|--------------------|--------------------|--------------------|
| Individual | BRL 95 | BRL 75 |
| ME/EPP/ICT/Microentrepreneur | BRL 95 | BRL 75 |
| Other legal entities | BRL 285 | BRL 220 |

> The GRU can be paid at any bank, lottery outlet, or via internet banking (barcode). The receipt must be attached to the system after payment to validate the filing.

### Step 6: Validate the filing and obtain the application number

Once payment is confirmed, the system automatically generates the **application number** in the format BR 10 2026 000000-0. This number is the official identifier of your process from then on.

From this point, you can track the progress of the application:

- Via **BuscaWeb** (public inquiry system)
- Via the new **Patent Services Module** (logged-in area with automatic notifications)

### Step 7: Manage the application in the Patent Services Module

This is the big news for 2026. On July 27, the INPI launched the test platform of the **Patent Services Module**, which offers:

- **My Workspace**: personalized area with your patent processes
- **Automatic email notifications**: alerts about required actions, publications, deadlines
- **Personalized mailbox**: direct communications from the INPI with guidance
- **Access sharing**: invite editors, viewers, and third parties to follow the process
- **Annuity tracking**: view upcoming annual fees to be paid

> ⚠️ In the first version, **filing and petitioning** are still done via e-Patentes. The Services Module is the management front-end, not the filing system. The INPI plans to integrate filing directly into the module in future versions.

## After filing: what to expect

### Publication of the application

The application is automatically published in the **Industrial Property Journal (RPI)** 18 months after the filing date (or the earliest priority date), unless a secrecy request is made. From publication onward, third parties can submit observations and opposition.

### Technical examination

The merit examination is carried out by an INPI patent examiner. The backlog is significant — see our guide on [priority patent processing]({{< relref "posts/guia-tramite-prioritario-patentes-inpi-2026/" >}}) to speed up the process.

The examiner evaluates:
- **Novelty** (Art. 11 of the LPI)
- **Inventive step** (Art. 13)
- **Industrial applicability** (Art. 15)
- **Descriptive sufficiency** (Art. 24)
- **Clarity of claims** (Art. 25)

If the examiner identifies irregularities, a **technical requirement** is issued, which you must respond to within the legal deadline (90 days, extendable by another 90).

### Grant or rejection

- If approved: the INPI publishes the grant decision in the RPI, and you have 60 days to pay the grant fee and the first annuity
- If rejected: an appeal to the INPI President is possible within 60 days

To find out the exact status of your process, consult our guide on [how to check the status of a process at the INPI]({{< relref "posts/como-consultar-andamento-processo-inpi/" >}}).

## Frequently asked questions

### Do I need a lawyer to file a patent?

It is not mandatory for an individual to file directly, but it is **highly recommended** to hire an industrial property agent. Drafting claims and following up on technical requirements require specialized knowledge that can make the difference between grant and rejection.

### Can I file a patent via the PCT?

Yes. International filing via the **Patent Cooperation Treaty (PCT)** allows you to seek protection in over 150 countries with a single application. Entry into the Brazilian national phase follows specific rules — see the [complete guide to PCT national phase entry in Brazil]({{< relref "posts/entrada-fase-nacional-pct-brasil-guia-2026/" >}}).

### Can software be patented in Brazil?

Software-implemented inventions **can** be patented in Brazil as long as they involve a technical effect — it is not enough to be pure computer software. For the difference between a software patent and computer program registration, see our [comparative guide]({{< relref "posts/patente-software-vs-registro-programa-computador-brasil/" >}}).

### How long does it take for a patent to be granted?

The average examination time at the INPI varies by technological field. In 2026, the INPI backlog for invention patents is approximately **5 to 8 years** for fields without priority processing. With priority processing, this period drops to 6 to 18 months.
