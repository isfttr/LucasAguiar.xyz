On July 27, 2026, the Brazilian Patent and Trademark Office (INPI) launched the test platform for its new **Patents Services Module** — a revamped interface designed to give patent applicants, attorneys, and holders a personalized workspace with automatic notifications, shared access management, and a streamlined dashboard for tracking patent proceedings. While patent filing itself still goes through the legacy **e-Patentes** system, the new module represents a concrete step forward in [INPI's automation roadmap]({{< relref "posts/inpi-automation-roadmap-2025-2029/" >}}).

This guide covers the entire patent filing process in Brazil in 2026 — from initial registration to publication and examination — including everything the new Patents Services Module changes for applicants.

## What is a patent and what does it protect?

Under Brazil's [Industrial Property Law (Law No. 9.279/96)](https://www.planalto.gov.br/ccivil_03/leis/l9279.htm) — the LPI — patents protect inventions that meet three criteria: novelty, inventive step, and industrial applicability. There are two types:

| Type | Protection | Duration | Example |
|------|-----------|----------|---------|
| **Invention Patent (PI)** | New product or process with inventive step and industrial application | 20 years from filing date | New chemical compound, manufacturing method |
| **Utility Model (MU)** | Functional improvement to a known object | 15 years from filing date | New tool shape improving ergonomics |

For a detailed comparison of all IP protection types in Brazil, see our guide on [types of intellectual property protection in Brazil]({{< relref "posts/ip-protection-types-brazil-complete-guide/" >}}).

## Prerequisites: what you need before you start

Gather these documents and information before opening the filing system:

1. **PAG registration** (INPI's user database) — mandatory for accessing any INPI system
2. **Technical documentation**: patent specification, claims, abstract, drawings (if applicable)
3. **GRU fee payment receipt** (federal payment slip) — initial filing fee
4. **Power of attorney** (if filing through a patent attorney)
5. **Priority document** (if claiming priority from an earlier filing — e.g., PCT application or Paris Convention filing)

> ⚠️ **Note:** Since 2025, INPI requires corporate applicants (CNPJ) and their attorneys to have a **gov.br account** at silver or gold level. Individual applicants (CPF) still use PAG username and password.

## Step-by-step: how to file a patent at INPI

### Step 1: Prior art search

Before filing, conduct a prior art search to verify your invention is novel. INPI offers search tools through BuscaWeb — see our [practical guide to patent searching at INPI]({{< relref "posts/busca-patentes-inpi-guia-pratico/" >}}). A proper search saves filing fees and helps draft stronger claims.

### Step 2: Access e-Patentes

Patent filing is done through the **e-Patentes** system at:

[https://www.gov.br/inpi/pt-br/servicos/patentes/deposito-de-patentes](https://www.gov.br/inpi/pt-br/servicos/patentes/deposito-de-patentes)

Log in with your PAG credentials (CPF + password) or gov.br account (for CNPJ holders and attorneys).

### Step 3: Fill in the application form

The electronic form asks for:

- **Applicant data**: name, CPF/CNPJ, address
- **Inventor data**: name and qualification of each inventor
- **Title of invention**: clear and descriptive, in Portuguese
- **Application type**: Invention Patent (PI) or Utility Model (MU)
- **IPC classification**: you can enter it or leave it for INPI — see our [IPC classification guide]({{< relref "posts/classificacao-internacional-patentes-ipc-guia-brasil/" >}})
- **Priority claim**: country, number, and date of earlier filing (if applicable)

### Step 4: Attach technical documents

The system accepts PDF, ODT, DOCX, and TXT files. Required documents:

| Document | Description | Format |
|----------|------------|--------|
| Specification | Complete and clear description of the invention | PDF/ODT |
| Claims | Defines the scope of protection | PDF/ODT |
| Abstract | Technical summary (up to 200 words) | PDF/ODT |
| Drawings (if any) | Figures, flowcharts, diagrams | PDF/TIF |
| Sequence listing (biotech) | For biotechnological inventions | TXT |

The **claims** are the most critical part — they define what you seek to protect. Draft them carefully, ideally with help from a [qualified patent search professional]({{< relref "posts/inpi-credenciamento-busca-anterioridade-patentes/" >}}).

### Step 5: Generate and pay the GRU fee

After submitting data and documents, the system generates a **GRU (Guia de Recolhimento da União)** — the federal fee payment slip. 2026 filing fees:

| Applicant type | Invention Patent (PI) | Utility Model (MU) |
|----------------|----------------------|--------------------|
| Individual | BRL 95 | BRL 75 |
| ME/EPP/ICT/Micro-entrepreneur | BRL 95 | BRL 75 |
| Other legal entities | BRL 285 | BRL 220 |

Pay the GRU at any bank, lottery outlet, or via internet banking. Upload the payment receipt to validate your filing.

### Step 6: Validate and get your application number

Once payment is confirmed, the system automatically generates the **application number** in the format BR 10 2026 000000-0. This is your official process identifier.

From this point, track your application through:
- **BuscaWeb** (public query system)
- The new **Patents Services Module** (logged-in workspace with automatic notifications)

### Step 7: Manage your application in the Patents Services Module

The big innovation of 2026. INPI launched the test platform of the **Patents Services Module** on July 27, offering:

- **My Workspace**: personalized area with your patent processes
- **Automatic email notifications**: alerts for required actions, publications, deadlines
- **Personalized mailbox**: direct communications from INPI with guidance
- **Access sharing**: invite editors, viewers, and third parties
- **Annuity tracking**: view upcoming annual fees

> ⚠️ In this first version, **filing and petitioning** are still done through e-Patentes. The Patents Services Module is the management front-end, not the filing system. INPI plans to integrate filing directly into the module in future releases.

## After filing: what to expect

### Publication

The application is automatically published in the **Industrial Property Journal (RPI)** 18 months after filing (or priority date), unless secrecy is requested. Third parties may submit observations and opposition after publication.

### Substantive examination

A patent examiner reviews the application for:
- **Novelty** (LPI Article 11)
- **Inventive step** (Article 13)
- **Industrial applicability** (Article 15)
- **Sufficient disclosure** (Article 24)
- **Claim clarity** (Article 25)

If issues arise, the examiner issues a **technical office action**, which must be responded to within 90 days (extendable by another 90).

### Grant or rejection

- If approved: INPI publishes the grant notice in the RPI, and you have 60 days to pay the grant fee and first annuity
- If rejected: you may appeal to the INPI President within 60 days

Need to speed things up? See our [guide to priority examination at INPI]({{< relref "posts/guia-tramite-prioritario-patentes-inpi-2026/" >}}).

## Frequently asked questions

### Do I need a lawyer to file a patent?

Individuals are not legally required to use a patent attorney, but it is **strongly recommended**. Drafting claims and responding to office actions require specialized knowledge that can determine grant or rejection.

### Can I file via PCT?

Yes. The **Patent Cooperation Treaty (PCT)** lets you seek protection in 150+ countries with a single international application. National phase entry in Brazil follows specific rules — see our [complete guide to PCT national phase entry in Brazil]({{< relref "posts/pct-national-phase-brazil-guide-2026/" >}}).

### How long does patent examination take?

Average examination time at INPI varies by technology field. In 2026, the backlog for invention patents is approximately **5 to 8 years** without priority examination. With priority examination, this drops to 6 to 18 months.
