On June 22, 2026, the 1st Specialized Panel of the Federal Regional Court
of the 2nd Region (TRF-2) unanimously confirmed the judgment rendered in Public Civil
Action No. 5095710-55.2021.4.02.5101/RJ, filed by the Brazilian Intellectual
Property Association (ABPI) against INPI and the Union. The decision represents a
milestone in the structuring of the agency and reverberates throughout the entire Brazilian
industrial property system.

## What the TRF-2 Decided

The panel, composed of Federal Judges Simone Schreiber (Rapporteur),
Cláudia Franco Corrêa and Júlio de Castilhos, fully upheld the original judgment.
The ruling recognizes the need for INPI's restructuring and establishes
a prospective path for the diagnosis and improvement of the agency, seeking
to ensure **material and financial conditions** for this purpose.

Throughout the trial — which had already been discussed in previous sessions — the
relevance of ABPI's work and its pioneering role in defending the Brazilian
industrial property system was highlighted.

## The history of the action

The Public Civil Action was filed in 2021, during the tenure of the then president of ABPI,
Luiz Edgard Montaury Pimenta. The central thesis was that INPI, despite institutional
advances in recent years, lacked the structural conditions to fully
exercise its constitutional and legal attributions.

The oral argument in the trial was led by Gabriel Leonardos, former president
of ABPI, who presented the technical argument on the need to strengthen
the agency as a condition for the legal certainty of the IP system in Brazil.
ABIFINA (Brazilian Association of Fine Chemicals, Biotechnology and Specialties Industries)
and ABAPI (Brazilian Association of Industrial Property Agents) acted as _amici curiae_.

## What it means in practice

The TRF-2 decision is not limited to the specific case. It establishes a **judicial
precedent** that recognizes the obligation of the Brazilian State to provide INPI with the
necessary means to perform its functions. This includes:

| Dimension | Impact |
|----------|---------|
| **Budgetary** | Pressure for an increase in the agency's budget, currently insufficient given the volume of requests |
| **People management** | Possibility of new public examinations and recomposition of the technical staff |
| **Technological infrastructure** | Acceleration of the automation and digitalization roadmap |
| **Predictability** | Greater security for system users — applicants, examiners, and investors |

For companies and startups that depend on the patent and trademark system, the signal is
clear: the Judiciary recognizes that a strengthened INPI is a condition for the
country's competitiveness. The decision adds to other recent advances, such as the
Backlog Combat Plan, the publication of examination guidelines, and the automation
roadmap 2025–2029.

## Connections with the current scenario

This decision comes at a time when INPI had already been accumulating institutional
achievements:

- The **[report on proposals for improving the LPI]({{< relref "posts/lpi-30-anos-propostas-aperfeicoamento-inpi" >}})** (June/2026), which
  consolidates internal suggestions for modernizing Law 9.279/1996
- The **[public consultation on patents for new uses in the chemical area]({{< relref
  "posts/inpi-diretrizes-segundo-uso-medico-patentes-quimica/" >}})**
  (2025-2026)
- The **2025–2029 automation roadmap**, which promises to digitally transform
  patent examination

However, the TRF-2 decision tackles the root of the problem: there is no point in
modernizing processes if the agency does not have the budget and personnel to execute them.
Judicial recognition of this reality is an important step, but implementation
will depend on the political will of the Executive and Legislative branches.

## Lessons for the innovation ecosystem

For startups, deep techs, and investors navigating the Brazilian IP system, the
decision brings two important messages:

1.  **The Judiciary is attentive to INPI's operations.** Decisions like this create
    jurisprudence that can be invoked in other actions — including claims for
    compensation due to delays in patent examination.

2.  **The strengthening of INPI is an agenda for all stakeholders.** ABPI,
    ABIFINA, and ABAPI have demonstrated that collective action can generate
    significant institutional changes. Companies that monitor these developments gain
    a strategic advantage.
