The Brazilian patent system offers three main mechanisms for third parties to challenge patent applications or granted patents: **pre-grant prior art submissions** (Art. 31 of the Brazilian IP Law — LPI), during examination; **administrative nullity** (Arts. 50-55), within the first six months after grant; and **judicial nullity actions** (Arts. 56-57), at any time during the patent's validity. Each has distinct deadlines, costs, legal grounds, and effects.

This guide consolidates all three mechanisms, their legal requirements, procedural steps, and strategies for choosing the most appropriate route.

If you are not yet familiar with the patent filing process, first read our [complete guide to filing a patent in Brazil]({{< relref "posts/how-to-file-patent-inpi-2026-guide/" >}}).

## Overview of Challenge Mechanisms

| Mechanism | Legal Basis | When to Use | Deadline | Who Can File | Cost |
|-----------|-----------|-------------|---------|--------------|------|
| **Pre-grant Submission (Subsídio)** | Art. 31, LPI | During technical examination | From publication until end of exam | Any interested party | Free (no specific fee) |
| **Administrative Nullity** | Arts. 50-55, LPI | After patent grant | Up to 6 months from grant | Any person with legitimate interest | Petition fee (code 272) |
| **Judicial Nullity** | Arts. 56-57, LPI | At any time | Throughout patent validity | INPI or any person with legitimate interest | Court costs + attorney fees |

## 1. Pre-grant Prior Art Submissions (Art. 31, LPI)

The pre-grant submission is the simplest and most accessible mechanism for third parties to influence patent examination. Anyone can submit documents and information to assist the INPI examiner in forming their technical opinion.

### Legal Basis

> **Art. 31.** Once the patent application is published and until the end of the examination, interested parties may submit documents and information to support the examination.
>
> **Sole paragraph.** The examination shall not begin before 60 (sixty) days have elapsed from the publication of the application.

### Key Characteristics

- **Who may submit:** any interested party — no need to demonstrate legitimate interest or be a party to the proceeding
- **When:** from publication in the RPI (Industrial Property Gazette, ~18 months after filing) until the final technical decision
- **What to submit:** prior art documents (patents, scientific articles, technical publications), technical arguments, expert reports
- **Cost:** no specific fee for submission (included under "Other Petitions" or free of charge)
- **Effect:** the examiner considers the submitted material, but the submitter does not become a party to the proceeding — there is no right to appeal the decision

### What to Include

A well-prepared pre-grant submission should contain:

1. **Application identification**: BR 10 20XX XXXXXX-X number
2. **Prior art documents**: copies of patents, articles, or publications showing the invention lacks novelty or inventive step
3. **Claim chart**: mapping between the application's claims and prior art documents, highlighting each disclosed technical feature
4. **Technical reasoning**: explanation of why the prior art anticipates or renders obvious the claimed invention
5. **Translations**: if the prior art is in a foreign language, simple translations of the most relevant excerpts are recommended

### How to File

The submission is filed through the INPI's electronic petition system (service 200 or directly via the Patent Services Module, depending on the current version). See our [guide on checking INPI process status]({{< relref "posts/como-consultar-andamento-processo-inpi/" >}}) for the petitioning interface.

> ⚠️ **Critical deadline:** the sole paragraph of Art. 31 prevents the examination from starting before 60 days from publication. This means there is a 60-day window right after RPI publication when the examiner has not yet begun their analysis — the best time to submit relevant prior art.

## 2. Administrative Nullity (Arts. 50 to 55, LPI)

Administrative nullity is the mechanism to challenge a granted patent directly at the INPI, without going to court.

### Legal Basis

> **Art. 50.** A patent shall be declared administratively null when:
>
> I — any of the legal requirements have not been met;
>
> II — the specification and claims do not comply with Arts. 24 and 25, respectively;
>
> III — the subject matter of the patent extends beyond the content of the originally filed application; or
>
> IV — any essential procedural formalities indispensable to grant have been omitted.
>
> **Art. 51.** Nullity proceedings may be initiated ex officio or upon request of any person with legitimate interest, within 6 (six) months from patent grant.

### Procedural Timeline

| Step | Deadline |
|------|---------|
| Nullity request filing | Up to 6 months from grant |
| Patent holder's response | 60 days from notice |
| INPI technical opinion | After holder's response |
| Parties' comments on opinion | 60 days (common deadline) |
| INPI President's decision | After comments |

### Who Has Legitimate Interest

The INPI does not require strict proof of legitimate interest at the filing stage, but consolidated administrative case law (CGREC Decision Collections) considers that:

- Direct competitors in the same market have presumed legitimate interest
- Trade associations have standing to defend collective interests
- The INPI may initiate nullity ex officio at any time (Art. 51, caput)

### Costs (INPI 2026 Fee Table)

Values vary by applicant size. Individuals, MEI, micro-enterprises, small businesses, ICTs, and non-profits receive 50% discounts. The complete fee table is available on the [INPI website](https://www.gov.br/inpi/pt-br/inpi-data/precificacao-dos-servicos/NovaTabeladeRetribuiesINPI_PortariaINPI_Final_20_dez_25.pdf).

| Service | Code | Full Price | With 50% Discount |
|---------|------|-----------|-------------------|
| Patent administrative nullity | 272 | ~BRL 800 | ~BRL 400 |
| Patent holder's response to nullity | 280 | ~BRL 800 | ~BRL 400 |

> Exact values depend on the current fee table update. Always check the [INPI costs page](https://www.gov.br/inpi/pt-br/servicos/custos-e-pagamento) for current prices.

### Effects

> **Art. 48.** Patent nullity shall produce effects as from the filing date of the application.

This means that if nullity is granted, the patent is considered as never having existed — effects retroact to the filing date. This is the most severe consequence, stronger than mere revocation.

## 3. Judicial Nullity Action (Arts. 56 and 57, LPI)

The judicial nullity action is the court-based mechanism to challenge a patent, with broader deadlines and greater legal certainty.

### Legal Basis

> **Art. 56.** A nullity action may be filed at any time during the patent's validity, by the INPI or any person with legitimate interest.
>
> **§ 1** Patent nullity may be argued at any time as a matter of defense.
>
> **§ 2** The judge may, preventively or incidentally, order the suspension of the patent's effects, subject to the applicable procedural requirements.
>
> **Art. 57.** The patent nullity action shall be filed in Federal Court, and the INPI, when not the plaintiff, shall intervene in the proceeding.

### Key Characteristics

- **Deadline:** any time during the patent's validity (unlike the 6-month administrative deadline)
- **Venue:** Federal Court (Federal District or plaintiff's/defendant's jurisdiction)
- **Parties:** plaintiff (any person with legitimate interest) × defendant (patent holder), with mandatory INPI intervention
- **Defense:** nullity may be raised as a defense in any lawsuit — even after the administrative deadline has expired
- **Effect:** final judgment → INPI publishes notice in the RPI for third-party awareness

### Administrative vs. Judicial Nullity

| Aspect | Administrative Nullity | Judicial Nullity |
|--------|----------------------|------------------|
| Deadline | 6 months from grant | Any time (during validity) |
| Cost | INPI fees (BRL 400-800) | Court costs + attorney fees |
| Complexity | Low to medium | High |
| Injunctive relief | No | Yes (preliminary injunction) |
| Final instance | INPI President (administrative) | Federal Appellate Court/Superior Courts (judicial) |
| Appeal available | Yes (new judicial nullity) | Yes (appeal, special appeal, extraordinary appeal) |

## 4. Appeal Against INPI Decisions

If the INPI rejects your patent application (or if you are a third party whose nullity request was denied), you may appeal to the INPI President.

### Deadlines and Procedure

- **Appeal deadline:** 60 days from the decision's publication in the RPI
- **Counter-arguments:** 60 days for the opposing party to respond
- **Reviewing body:** General Coordination of Appeals and Administrative Nullity Proceedings (CGREC) — under the INPI Presidency
- **Final decision:** issued by the INPI President, closing the administrative instance

The CGREC maintains periodic publications with consolidated case law. The Administrative Second Instance Decision Collections for Patents (2017-2023 editions) are available on the [INPI Resources and Nullity page](https://www.gov.br/inpi/pt-br/servicos/recursos-e-nulidades) and are essential references for understanding the INPI's consolidated positions.

## 5. Quick Reference: Which Route to Choose?

| Scenario | Recommended Route | Reason |
|----------|-----------------|--------|
| Application still under examination | Pre-grant Submission (Art. 31) | Fastest, free, no legitimate interest required |
| Patent granted less than 6 months ago | Administrative Nullity | Cheaper and faster than judicial route |
| Patent granted more than 6 months ago | Judicial Nullity | Administrative deadline has expired |
| Urgency (risk of imminent harm) | Judicial Nullity + Injunction | Possible preliminary suspension of effects |
| Defense in infringement lawsuit | Nullity as Defense | Art. 56, §1 — available at any time |
| Multiple patents by same holder | Combine Submission + Administrative Nullity | Submissions for pending applications + nullity for granted ones |

## 6. Practical Strategies

### For Patent Holders (How to Defend)

- **Monitor the RPI weekly** after your application is published — any third party may submit prior art
- **Respond to submissions politely** — the examiner considers the material, but you can present counter-arguments during examination
- **Keep your application in good standing** (annuity fees paid, office actions answered on time) — poorly maintained patents are easy nullity targets
- **Consider priority examination** to accelerate grant — see our [complete guide to priority examination]({{< relref "posts/guia-tramite-prioritario-patentes-inpi-2026/" >}})

### For Third Parties (How to Challenge)

- **Conduct a thorough prior art search** — our [practical guide to patent searching at the INPI]({{< relref "posts/busca-patentes-inpi-guia-pratico/" >}}) shows how to find relevant documents
- **Document everything** — every statement must be supported by documentary evidence
- **Prefer pre-grant submissions** if the application is still under examination — it's free and requires no legitimate interest
- **Act quickly on administrative nullity** — the 6-month deadline from grant is short and non-extendable

## 7. Frequently Asked Questions

### Does a pre-grant submission suspend the examination?

No. The examiner considers the submitted material alongside the examination, but the proceeding is not suspended. The only restriction is that examination cannot begin before 60 days from publication (Art. 31, sole paragraph).

### Can I submit prior art anonymously?

Yes. Art. 31 refers to "interested parties" without requiring full identification. However, anonymous submissions may carry less weight, as the examiner cannot request clarifications or additional documents.

### Does administrative nullity prevent a judicial action?

No. The INPI President's administrative decision can be challenged in court. Additionally, a judicial nullity action may be filed independently — even if an administrative decision has already been issued.

### How much does a judicial nullity action cost?

Costs vary significantly: attorney fees, court costs (proportional to the claim value), and technical expert reports (when needed). For complex cases, total costs may range from BRL 30,000 to BRL 150,000 or more. Consult a specialized IP attorney for accurate estimates.
