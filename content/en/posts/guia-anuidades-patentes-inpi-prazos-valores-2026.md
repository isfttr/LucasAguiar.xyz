Keeping a patent alive in Brazil costs money every year. The annuity (or annual fee) is the maintenance fee that the holder must pay to the INPI to prevent the extinction of the application or granted patent. Non-payment is the number one cause of patent extinction in Brazil — and it is also the most avoidable.

This guide consolidates everything you need to know about patent annuities at the INPI in 2026: when they become due, how much they cost per year and per patent type, how to pay, what to do if you miss the deadline, and how the new Patent Services Module (in testing starting July 27, 2026) will simplify the process.

## What are patent annuities and why do they exist

The annuity is the annual fee due to the INPI to keep the patent application or granted patent in force. It is provided for in art. 84 of the Industrial Property Law (Law 9.279/96 — LPI):

> **Art. 84.** The applicant of the application and the patent holder are obliged to pay the annual fee, starting from the beginning of the third year from the filing date.

The value is progressive: it increases as the patent ages, reflecting the growing economic benefit that a mature patent can generate. The annuity system exists in practically all patent offices worldwide (USPTO, EPO, JPO) as a way to fund the maintenance of the system and encourage the abandonment of low commercial value patents.

## When annuities become due

Annuities are due **from the 3rd year (24th month) of the patent application filing date** — not from grant. This means you may be paying annuities for an application that is still in the examination queue.

| Milestone | Deadline |
|-------|-------|
| 1st annuity | Due in the 3rd year of filing (24 months after filing) |
| Subsequent annuities | Each year, on the same anniversary date of the filing |
| Duration of obligation | Until the end of the term: 20 years (PI) or 15 years (MU) from filing |

Important: the annuity is due even if the application has not yet been examined. The INPI backlog (which can take 5 to 9 years for examination to begin) does not suspend the obligation to pay annuities.

## Grace periods, restoration, and extinction

The INPI offers a tolerance window for delays, but prolonged carelessness leads to definitive extinction of the patent.

### Ordinary term

The annuity must be paid by the due date (filing date + number of years). Payment can be made up to 6 months before the due date.

### Extraordinary term (art. 86, §1 of the LPI)

If not paid within the ordinary term, the annuity can be paid with a 50% surcharge on the value, within 6 months after the due date:

| Situation | GRU Code | Value |
|----------|-----------|-------|
| Annuity within ordinary term | 220 | Full value |
| Annuity within extraordinary term (up to 6 months late) | 221 | Value + 50% |

### Restoration (art. 87 of the LPI)

If the extraordinary term is also missed, the INPI publishes the shelving decision (code 8.6 in the RPI). From then on, the holder has **3 months** to request the restoration of the application's progress (GRU 208) and pay the overdue annuity.

After these 3 months, if no restoration is made, the INPI publishes the definitive shelving (decision 8.11) — the patent is extinguished and its content falls into the public domain.

| Stage | Deadline | Consequence |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| Ordinary payment | By the due date | — |
| Extraordinary payment | Up to 6 months after due date | 50% surcharge |
| Shelving (decision 8.6) | After 6 months without payment | Process shelved |
| Restoration | Up to 3 months from decision 8.6 | GRU 208 + annuity payment |
| Definitive shelving (decision 8.11) | After 3 months without restoration | Patent extinction — public domain |

## How to pay: from PAG to the new Patent Services Module

The INPI offers two payment methods, with the arrival of the new Patent Services Module in August 2026.

### PAG System (current)

The PAG system (available at [pag.inpi.gov.br](https://pag.inpi.gov.br/)) allows you to generate the GRU for any patent service.

1. Access PAG with your gov.br login
2. Select "Patents" and the desired service code (220, 224, etc.)
3. Fill in the process number
4. Generate the GRU
5. Pay at any bank (barcode)

PAG will remain available during the transition period.

### New Patent Services Module (starting August 2026)

The INPI launched the second version of the Patent Services Module (in testing from July 27 to August 14, 2026, with exclusive operation starting August 19). Among the new features is **integrated annuity management**:

- The system guides the user in issuing the GRU corresponding to the due annuity
- In the personalized workspace, it is possible to consult a table with annuity deadlines
- The module's mailbox sends advance payment reminders
- The system also provides guidance on the restoration fee when necessary

For offices managing hundreds of processes, this functionality significantly reduces the risk of missing deadlines.

See full details in the post about the [new INPI Patent Services Module]({{< relref "posts/inpi-modulo-servicos-patentes-novas-funcionalidades-2026/" >}}).

### Practical recommendations

- **Payment schedule:** mark on the calendar the anniversary date of each patent's filing. Consider paying 30 days in advance to avoid banking surprises.
- **Chronological order:** the INPI does not send individual payment reminders. The responsibility is 100% on the holder.
- **Consolidation:** if you have multiple patents, centralize control in a spreadsheet or IP management system. The new INPI Module promises to facilitate this monitoring with a personalized workspace.
- **Power of attorney:** if you use an IP agent, they can manage payments — but the final legal responsibility always lies with the holder.
- **Category change:** if your company has changed size (e.g., from ME to large PJ), the annuity values change. Update your registration with the INPI.

## Term of validity × number of annuities

Many people get confused: an invention patent lasts 20 years from filing, but you pay 18 annuities (from the 3rd to the 20th year). This is because the first two years are covered by the filing fee.

For utility models (15-year term), there are 13 annuities (from the 3rd to the 15th year).

| Type | Term | Annuities to pay | Period |
|------|----------|-------------------|---------|
| Invention Patent (PI) | 20 years | 18 | 3rd to 20th year |
| Utility Model (MU) | 15 years | 13 | 3rd to 15th year |
| Certificate of Addition (CAd) | Until the end of the PI | Variable | Linked to the main PI |

## Differences between application annuity and granted patent annuity

While the application is under examination (not granted), the annuity paid is the "patent application" annuity (code 220). After grant, the annuity is the "patent" annuity (codes 224 to 229). The values for the granted patent are higher than those for the application.

This means that in the year of grant, if the patent is granted after the application annuity has been paid, it may be necessary to top up to the patent annuity — check with the INPI or your agent.

## Frequently asked questions

### Do I need to pay annuities during the INPI backlog?

Yes. The obligation to pay annuities begins in the 3rd year of filing, regardless of whether the technical examination has started or not. The backlog does not suspend the obligation.

### Can I pay several annuities at once?

Yes, there is no legal impediment to paying multiple annuities in advance. The PAG system allows you to generate GRUs for whatever period you wish.

### Is the annuity deductible from income tax?

Yes. Patent annuities are deductible operating expenses for legal entities taxed under the real profit regime. Consult your accountant.

### What happens if I miss the restoration deadline?

The patent is definitively extinguished (shelving 8.11). The content falls into the public domain and cannot be recovered. The only recourse is judicial, and chances are remote.

### Will the new module charge more?

No. The new Patent Services Module is a digital platform — the fees (GRU) are the same as in the PAG system. The advantage lies in integrated management and automatic reminders.
