On Tuesday (June 30), the Federal Senate approved **Bill 2.583/2020**, which establishes the **National Health Strategy for the Economic-Industrial Health Complex (CEIS)** as the legal framework for the sector's industrial policy. The text, which had already passed the Chamber of Deputies in July 2025, now proceeds for presidential sanction.

The bill was authored by Congressman **Doutor Luizinho (PP/RJ)** and reported in the Senate by Senator **Rogério Carvalho (PT/SE)**, who emphasized in plenary that the proposal "fills a gap in legislation on the matter" by transforming into law what until now was supported only by infra-legal norms.

---

## What Bill 2.583/2020 establishes

The bill creates the **National Health Strategy**, a set of guidelines and instruments aimed at encouraging the national production of medicines, vaccines, equipment, and medical supplies essential to the Unified Health System (SUS).

Among the established **guidelines** are:

- Strengthening the SUS and ensuring access to health technologies
- Training human resources for the sector
- Preventing and combating epidemics
- Encouraging the national production of medicines and medical devices
- International integration of strategic Brazilian companies
- Using the State's purchasing power to stimulate local production

---

## Strategic Health Company (EES) — the core of the new framework

One of the central elements of the Bill is the creation of the legal entity of the **Strategic Health Company (EES)**. To qualify as an EES, the company must meet minimum conditions, such as:

- Have industrial facilities in Brazil for manufacturing **strategic health products (PES)**
- Present a history of productive activity and innovation
- Have the capacity to ensure production continuity and expansion in the country

Accredited companies will have access to **significant incentives**:

1.  **Priority in regulatory procedures** — registrations, licenses, and authorizations with priority treatment
2.  **Priority in public calls** for research, development, and innovation
3.  **Facilitated access to BNDES credit lines** — with competitive rates, adjustable terms, and grace periods
4.  **Differentiated import tariffs**, compatible with the competitiveness of national industry

Accreditation will be carried out by an act of the Executive Power, which may disaccredit the company in case of risks to national sovereignty or the supply of the SUS. There will also be permanent monitoring of prices practiced in national and international markets.

---

## Why this matters — the problem of external dependence

Brazil imports about **90% of active pharmaceutical ingredients (APIs)** used in the production of medicines, and the health sector's trade deficit exceeds **US$ 10 billion annually**. Dependence is especially critical for highly complex medicines — oncological, biological — and high-tech equipment, such as CT scanners and MRI machines.

The COVID-19 pandemic dramatically exposed this vulnerability. The country faced shortages of ventilators, PPE, and active ingredients for vaccines, and struggled with logistical difficulties in acquiring essential raw materials. It was in this context that Bill 2.583/2020 was presented in May 2020.

## The processing — six years until approval

Bill 2.583/2020 had a long processing period, passing through committees, replacing rapporteurs, and accumulating opinions over six years:

| Date | Event |
|------|--------|
| **12/05/2020** | Presentation in the Chamber by Rep. Doutor Luizinho (PP/RJ) |
| 2021-2024 | Reports by Juscelino Filho, Alexandre Padilha, and Clodoaldo Magalhães; public hearings |
| **08/07/2025** | **Approved in the Chamber** — 316 votes in favor, 110 against |
| **30/06/2026** | **Approved in the Senate** — Rapporteur Rogério Carvalho (PT/SE) |
| **2026** | **Proceeds for presidential sanction** |

## The relationship with Nova Indústria Brasil (NIB)

Bill 2.583/2020 is not an isolated piece. It is part of **Nova Indústria Brasil (NIB)**, the federal government's industrial policy plan that foresees R$ 300 billion in investments until 2033. **Mission 2** of the NIB is precisely "to expand autonomy in the production of medicines, vaccines, health equipment and supplies" — the CEIS.

What the Bill does is transform this mission — and the set of CEIS policies — from initiatives based on infra-legal norms (subject to revision by decree) into **a stable legislative commitment**. As Senator Rogério Carvalho highlighted, this stability is what allows the private sector, national and international, to envision a long-term horizon for investment.

---

## Next steps

The text approved in the Senate now proceeds to the **Palácio do Planalto**, where President Lula may sanction it in full or veto specific provisions. Given that the government is the proponent of the NIB and that the rapporteur in the Senate is from the allied base, full sanction is the most likely scenario.

If approved without vetoes, the new law will take effect immediately, paving the way for the accreditation of the first Strategic Health Companies and the operationalization of the foreseen incentives.

---

## What to watch going forward

The approval of Bill 2.583/2020 is a milestone, but implementation will be the real test. Some points deserve attention:

- **Regulation**: the Executive Power will need to define objective criteria for the accreditation of EES and for the list of strategic health products
- **Budget**: incentives (BNDES credit, differentiated tariffs) depend on budgetary allocations and financing capacity
- **Price monitoring**: the price monitoring mechanism will be essential to prevent distortions
- **Articulation with patent policy**: national drug production involves intellectual property issues that the Bill does not directly address

Brazil has already demonstrated, in critical moments such as the pandemic, that the capacity to internally produce health supplies is a matter of sovereignty. It remains to be seen whether the legal framework now approved will be sufficient to transform this capacity into reality.
