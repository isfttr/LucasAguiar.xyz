There are only a few days left for those who want to participate in the **5th CSD-ABPI Moot**, Brazil’s only arbitration competition exclusively dedicated to Intellectual Property. Registrations have been extended until **July 8, 2026** — an additional week from the original deadline, responding to requests from teams that were unable to finalize their lineup in time.

Organized by the Dispute Resolution Center of the Brazilian Intellectual Property Association (CSD-ABPI), the competition takes place on **August 15 and 16, 2026**, at the offices of Mattos Filho in Rio de Janeiro (Praia do Flamengo, 200 — 11th floor).

## The 2026 edition case

In this edition, participants will face a fictitious dispute between companies in the cosmetics sector, involving:

- Allegations of trademark infringement
- Conflicts over **product trade dress**
- Unfair competition
- Debates on **arbitral jurisdiction** to analyze the matter

The case is particularly timely and reflects contemporary legal challenges at the intersection of intellectual property, business contracts, arbitration, and the creative economy. Topics such as coexistence and lapse of distinctive signs, limits of the arbitration clause, the competence-competence principle, and the interaction between arbitration and the Judiciary in IP disputes are at the heart of the dispute.

The full document with the fictitious case is already available on the competition website, and the deadline for clarification requests runs until **July 9, 2026**.

## Who can participate

**Teams of students, law firms, and academic groups** interested in arbitration and intellectual property may register. The competition is inspired by renowned international competitions of its kind and is now in its fifth edition, with previous editions held in 2021, 2022, 2023, and 2025.

More than a competition, the CSD-ABPI Moot is an opportunity to:

- Deepen knowledge in arbitration applied to IP
- Develop **oral argumentation** skills
- Experience, in a practical and qualified manner, the challenges of resolving intellectual property disputes
- Network with professionals and arbitrators in the field

## Schedule and connection with the ABPI Global Meeting

The competition precedes the **ABPI Global Innovation and Intellectual Property Meeting — ABPI 2026**, the largest IP congress in Latin America, taking place from **August 16 to 18** also in Rio de Janeiro. The competition schedule has been adjusted so that participants can also attend the **Young IP Professional Day**, which takes place on Sunday, August 16, starting at 1:30 PM.

For those interested in arbitration and IP, the combined schedule offers a full week of hands-on learning and networking with leading names in the sector.

## Why this matters

Arbitration has become an increasingly relevant mechanism for resolving intellectual property disputes in Brazil. Unlike the Judiciary, arbitration offers confidentiality, speed, and the possibility of choosing specialized arbitrators — advantages that are particularly important in cases involving trade secrets, sensitive technologies, and high-value trademarks.

ABPI, through its Dispute Resolution Center, has played an active role in promoting arbitration as an alternative route for IP litigation. In a landmark decision, the TRF2 recognized the importance of institutional strengthening of the Brazilian IP system — a decision that also impacts the dispute resolution environment in the sector.

## How to register

Registrations run until **July 8, 2026** and must be made through the official ABPI website. The fictitious case, the full rules, and details about evaluating arbitrators are available on the competition page.
