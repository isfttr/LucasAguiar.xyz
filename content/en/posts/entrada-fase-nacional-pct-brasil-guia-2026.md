In 2025, international patent applications under the Patent Cooperation Treaty (PCT) grew by 0.7% and reached 275,900 filings worldwide. Digital communication technologies (+6.1%) and semiconductors (+6.1%) led the growth rates among the top ten technology fields, according to data released by WIPO in March 2026.

For inventors and companies filing a PCT application and wishing to protect their invention in Brazil, the next step is the **national phase entry** — the process of converting the international application into a Brazilian patent application before the INPI. This guide covers everything you need to know: deadlines, documents, fees, procedures, and common pitfalls.

## What is the PCT and why the national phase exists

The Patent Cooperation Treaty (PCT) is an international treaty administered by WIPO that allows filing a single international patent application with effect in over 150 contracting states. It does not grant patents — that is done by national offices (such as the INPI), after national phase entry.

The system has two phases:

| Phase | Description | Deadline |
|-------|-------------|----------|
| **International phase** | PCT application filing, international search, publication (18 months), preliminary examination (optional) | 0 to 30 months from priority |
| **National phase** | Conversion of the PCT application into a national application in each country where protection is sought | Up to 30 months from priority (standard) |

Brazil has been a signatory to the PCT since 1978 (Decree No. 81.742/1978) and national phase entry is regulated by articles 21 to 27 of the Industrial Property Law (Law 9.279/96) and by INPI/PR Resolution No. 294/2022.

## Deadline: 30 months from priority

The standard deadline for national phase entry in Brazil is **30 months from the international filing date** (earliest priority, if any). This is the deadline established by Article 22(1) of the PCT, adopted by the INPI.

**Attention:** The deadline is non-extendable. If the 30-month deadline is missed, the PCT application loses effect in Brazil. There is no possibility of restoration — except only in cases of force majeure duly proven (Art. 22(3) PCT).

For PCT applications with priority claim (Art. 4 of the Paris Convention), count the 30 months from the earliest priority date, not from the PCT filing date.

## Documents required for national phase entry

When entering the national phase at the INPI, the applicant must submit:

1. **Petition for national phase entry** — electronic form in the INPI e-Patentes or e-PCT system
2. **Description, claims, drawings, and abstract** — translated into Portuguese, except for exceptions provided in specific resolutions
3. **Power of attorney** — if a representative is appointed (not required for initial filing, but required for subsequent acts such as complying with office actions)
4. **Proof of payment of the basic fee** (GRU) — specific code for national phase entry
5. **Proof of payment of priority deposit** — when applicable (if there is a priority claim)
6. **Assignment document** — if the PCT applicant is not the inventor and ownership needs to be proven

### Translation requirement

All content of the PCT application (description, claims, drawings with text, abstract) must be submitted in Portuguese. The translation can be done by a sworn translator or a public translator, and the INPI does not require specific certification, but it is recommended to have the translation done by a qualified professional to avoid errors that could compromise the scope of protection.

## Step-by-step of national phase entry

### 1. Check the deadline

Confirm the earliest priority date of your PCT application and calculate the 30-month limit. It is recommended to start the process **at least 60 days in advance** to avoid issues with translation, obtaining documents, and payments.

### 2. Prepare the translation

Translate into Brazilian Portuguese:
- Description (specification)
- Claims
- Abstract
- Texts in drawings
- Any other textual documents accompanying the application

### 3. Access the INPI e-PCT system

Since 2025, the INPI offers the e-PCT module integrated into the e-Patentes system. Access is via [gov.br](https://www.gov.br/inpi/pt-br/servicos/patentes), with login using a gov.br account at silver or gold level.

### 4. Fill out the entry form

In the system, select the option "Entrada na Fase Nacional de Pedido PCT". Provide:
- International application number (PCT/XX2025/XXXXXX)
- International filing date
- Applicant and inventor names
- Claimed priorities (if any)
- Title of the invention in Portuguese

### 5. Attach the documents

Attach the files in formats accepted by the INPI (PDF/A for textual documents, XML for structured data when applicable).

### 6. Issue and pay the GRU

The Union Collection Guide (GRU) is issued within the INPI system itself. The specific fee code for national phase entry of PCT applications is **200** (patent filing) or a specific code for PCT, according to the current table.

### 7. File the application

After payment, the system generates an INPI case number (BR 10 2026 XXXXXXX-X). Keep this number — it is the identifier of your Brazilian patent application.

## What happens after entry

Once the national phase entry is filed, the application follows the normal procedure of a Brazilian patent application:

1. **Publication** — 18 months after filing (or from the earliest priority), the application is published in the Revista da Propriedade Industrial (RPI)
2. **Request for examination** — must be requested within 36 months from the filing date (Art. 33 LPI). Without a request for examination, the application is shelved.
3. **Technical examination** — the INPI analyzes patentability: novelty, inventive step, and industrial application (Arts. 8-15 LPI)
4. **Compliance with office actions** — if the examiner points out irregularities, the applicant has 60 or 90 days to respond
5. **Grant or rejection** — at the end of the examination, the INPI grants or rejects the patent
6. **Annuities** — mandatory annual payment from the 3rd year (Art. 84 LPI), under penalty of forfeiture

## Common pitfalls (and how to avoid them)

### Missed deadline

The main cause of loss of rights in the PCT in Brazil is failure to meet the 30-month deadline. The INPI **does not accept late entry petitions**. Active monitoring and a deadline calendar from the PCT filing are recommended.

### Inaccurate translation

Translation errors in the claims can reduce or change the scope of protection. Unlike some countries, the INPI does not accept significant corrections after filing — the translation submitted at national phase entry is binding.

### Lack of request for examination

The request for examination must be made within 36 months of filing. Many applicants miss this deadline due to lack of monitoring. Unlike the 30-month deadline for entry, the examination deadline can be reinstated in some cases, but with additional costs and risks.

### Payment of annuities

Annuities start counting from the 3rd year of filing, but are due even during examination. Non-payment for more than 6 months leads to definitive shelving.

## Frequently asked questions

**Do I need a representative in Brazil for national phase entry?**
It is not mandatory for the initial petition, but highly recommended. A qualified representative can receive notifications, comply with office actions, and perform all procedural acts. The INPI accepts powers of attorney in Portuguese or English.

**Can I enter the national phase before 30 months?**
Yes. Early entry is permitted and can speed up examination. Simply submit the complete documentation and pay the fees before the deadline.

**Does the INPI accept PCT applications in English?**
No. The documentation must be in Portuguese. The translation can be submitted at the time of entry or, in exceptional cases, upon commitment to submit later (with additional costs).

**What if I only want to protect in Brazil, without the PCT?**
In that case, direct patent filing at the INPI (national application via the Paris Convention) is simpler and cheaper. The PCT is advantageous when seeking protection in multiple countries.

**Does the prioritized patent examination apply to PCT applications?**
Yes. PCT applications in the national phase can request prioritized examination (INPI/PR Resolution No. 283/2021) if they fall under eligible categories, such as green technologies, public health, or certain ICT areas. In July 2026, the INPI resumed prioritized examination for patents in class H04 (Electric Communication Technique).
