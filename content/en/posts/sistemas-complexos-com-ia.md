I don't know what your algorithm is like, but on mine, aside from the Iran war and Brazilian politics, there are many topics related to artificial intelligence. Many of them talk about new tools, models, and workflows, and just as many talk more critically about this usage.

I noticed a trend that I found interesting to share with you.

Over the past year, Amazon experienced several blackouts in its services, both on the Amazon website itself and on AWS. Amazon is one of the companies that has most encouraged the use of artificial intelligence by its workers, and, by all indications, these blackouts were a consequence of this growing use within the company, to the point that there was eventually a limitation on incorporating this code into production – now a senior engineer has to evaluate it before it is incorporated.

However, if we stop to think about the amount of code lines produced by each of the engineers below that senior, the review of all that code will inevitably also have to be assisted by artificial intelligence. Nothing new there. We already have businesses that have been offering solutions specifically for this for some time.

Now, let's jump to something else very complex that seemingly has nothing to do with this scenario. Humans, and animals in general, possess super complex characteristics that are the basis for the entire health sector, including doctors, pharmaceutical companies, and so on. Why are they necessary? Because they need to deal with super complex systems that are only partially understood so far. The other day I was studying amyloidosis and how protein misfolding can lead to dire consequences in humans. This is just one class of diseases whose mechanism is difficult to treat, one of the strategies for which is gene editing. The point is that over time, we have been discovering the gigantic complexity of biological systems and have worked, through understanding, to build tools that improve our quality of life and cure diseases.

All this is to say: will we, in the future, due to the progressive employment of AI by developers, come to have a scenario where they become akin to scientists who need to understand code and seek solutions that “cure” problems created by the complexity of AIs?

Because it is clear that systems more complex than what we currently have tend indeed to become incomprehensible to humans in the future, in the same way that biological systems were practically incomprehensible to us some time ago.
