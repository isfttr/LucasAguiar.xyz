**4 MIN READ**

On June 22, 2026, the Brazilian Intellectual Property Association (ABPI) inaugurated the Peter Dirk Siemsen auditorium at its headquarters in Rio de Janeiro, in posthumous homage to one of the greatest names in the history of Brazilian intellectual property. The ceremony took place days after Siemsen's passing, marking the end of an era for the IP community in the country.

More than a simple tribute, the inauguration of the auditorium symbolizes the recognition of a trajectory that intertwines with the very history of industrial property in Brazil.

## Who was Peter Dirk Siemsen

Lawyer and businessman, Peter Dirk Siemsen was the founder of Dannemann, Siemsen, Bigler & Ipanema Moreira — one of the most important and traditional intellectual property law firms in Latin America. His work, however, transcended legal practice to encompass the institutional construction of the Brazilian IP system.

Siemsen was one of the founders of ABPI, an association of which he became Honorary President, and is remembered for transforming the association into the Brazilian group of AIPPI (International Association for the Protection of Intellectual Property), strengthening Brazil's international presence in the field. He himself became president of AIPPI, consolidating his global influence.

His greatest recognition came with his induction into the **Intellectual Property Hall of Fame** — the Intellectual Property Hall of Fame — of which he is **the only Brazilian member**. The distinction, created by IAM (Intellectual Asset Management), honors individuals who have made exceptional contributions to the development of intellectual property on a global scale.

## A career of global leadership

Peter Dirk Siemsen's trajectory is marked by an impressive succession of leadership positions in national and international organizations:

- **President of AIPPI** (International Association for the Protection of Intellectual Property) — the most important international IP association
- **Vice-President of the Intellectual Property Commission of the ICC** (International Chamber of Commerce)
- **ICC Regional Ambassador for Latin America**
- **Honorary Member of FICPI** (International Federation of Intellectual Property Attorneys)
- **Arbitrator of the WIPO Arbitration and Mediation Center** (World Intellectual Property Organization)
- **Arbitrator of CAS** (Court of Arbitration for Sport)
- Member of institutions such as **INTA**, **AIPLA**, **GRUR**, **MARQUES**, **LES**, **ATRIP**, and **ECTA**
- Member of the **Board of Directors of Amcham/RJ**

Beyond law, Siemsen was a passionate sailor, having been **Brazilian, South American, and world champion in the veteran category**, as well as an international and Olympic judge for the sport — a demonstration of his versatility and discipline.

## The legacy for Brazilian IP

The importance of Peter Dirk Siemsen for intellectual property in Brazil cannot be underestimated. His work took place during a period of transformation of the Brazilian IP system — from the promulgation of the Industrial Property Code (Law 5.772/71) to the promulgation of the current Industrial Property Law (Law 9.279/96, the LPI), which turns 30 in 2026 and was the subject of a recent INPI report.

As the author of several articles on industrial property and technology transfer in Brazil, Siemsen actively contributed to the training of generations of IP professionals. His firm, Dannemann Siemsen, trained some of the country's most respected patent and trademark specialists.

His son, **Peter Eduardo Siemsen**, current president of ABPI, summarized the sentiment during the auditorium's inauguration ceremony:

> "My father's story intertwines with the history of ABPI and industrial property in Brazil. His inspiration keeps alive the determination of many of us."

## The Peter Dirk Siemsen Auditorium

The inauguration ceremony was attended by Elisabeth Siemsen, her grandson Eric, and authorities such as **Schmuell Lopes Cantanhed** (Director of the WIPO Office in Brazil), **Renata Lisboa** (INTA consultant), and **Maria Beatriz Dellore** (USPTO regional consultant).

All participants received a copy of the book published in honor of Peter Dirk Siemsen, which records his contribution to the development of intellectual property.

## What this means for the IP market

The passing of Peter Dirk Siemsen marks the end of a cycle in the history of Brazilian intellectual property. He belonged to a generation of pioneers who not only practiced IP law but also built the institutions that today sustain the system.

For lawyers, IP agents, and companies that rely on the patent and trademark system, his legacy is a reminder of the importance of **institutional building** — ABPI, AIPPI, and the other associations he helped strengthen continue to be central actors in the formulation of IP policies in Brazil and worldwide.

At a time when INPI is undergoing a historic restructuring (confirmed by the recent decision of the TRF-2), Siemsen's trajectory serves as a reference for what it means to build a solid and internationally respected IP system.
