Registering a trademark with the INPI is the first step to protecting a distinctive sign in Brazil. What many owners do not know is that the registration can be extinguished — even before the end of the 10-year validity period — if the trademark is not used. This mechanism is called **cancellation for non-use** and is provided for in articles 142 to 146 of the Industrial Property Law (Law No. 9,279/96).

This guide covers everything you need to know about trademark cancellation at the INPI: when it can be requested, by whom, the cost, the step-by-step process, how to defend yourself, and how to avoid losing the registration due to non-use.

## What is Trademark Cancellation for Non-Use?

Cancellation for non-use is the extinction of a trademark registration due to lack of use. Unlike nullity (which annuls the registration due to a defect from the outset, such as conflict with a prior trademark), cancellation penalizes the owner who **does not use the trademark in Brazil** within the legal deadlines.

The legislator's logic is simple: there is no point in keeping an active registration that blocks third parties from using similar signs if the owner is not using the trademark. Registration exists to protect use in the marketplace, not to reserve signs indefinitely.

> ⚠️ **Important:** Cancellation for non-use does not happen automatically. Someone (individual or legal entity) must **request** it from the INPI. If nobody asks, the registration remains active even without use — but this is not advisable, as any competitor can request it at any time.

## Legal Basis: Articles 142 to 146 of the LPI

Trademark cancellation for non-use is governed by the following articles of Law No. 9,279/96:

| Article | Content |
|---------|---------|
| **Art. 142, III** | The registration is extinguished by cancellation for non-use |
| **Art. 143** | The registration shall lapse if, after 5 years from grant, use has not begun in Brazil or has been interrupted for more than 5 consecutive years |
| **Art. 143, §1** | Cancellation shall not occur if the owner justifies the non-use for legitimate reasons |
| **Art. 144** | The request for cancellation may be filed by any person with a legitimate interest, upon payment of the fee |
| **Art. 145** | The owner will be notified to respond within **60 days**, otherwise cancellation will be decreed |
| **Art. 146** | The owner may present evidence of use or justifications for non-use |

The administrative regulation of the procedure is found in **INPI/PR Resolution No. 249/2018** and **INPI/PR Ordinance No. 79/2022**, which establish the procedural rules.

## Deadlines and Requirements

### 5-year term from grant

The request for cancellation can only be filed **5 years after the date of registration grant**. The starting point is the date of issuance of the registration certificate, not the filing date of the application.

Example: a mark granted in March 2021 can only have its registration challenged for non-use from March 2026.

### When cancellation can be requested

There are two situations provided for in Art. 143:

| Situation | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Non-initiation of use** | After 5 years from grant, the owner has not begun using the mark in Brazil |
| **Interruption of use** | The owner used the mark but interrupted use for more than 5 consecutive years |

### Who can request

Any person with a **legitimate interest** may file the request. In practice, the requester is almost always a competitor or third party interested in registering a similar sign that finds the mark as an obstacle during INPI examination.

The INPI considers interest to be presumed when the requester has a trademark application rejected based on the challenged mark, or when they demonstrate activity in the same field.

### Use that does not prevent cancellation

The INPI considers as **effective use** the use of the mark as registered, on products or services actually placed on the market. The following are not considered valid use:

- Merely symbolic use (to "hold" the registration)
- Use only in internal materials (letterhead, invoices) without commercialization
- Use on an insignificant scale with no real commercial activity
- Use on products or services different from those specified in the registration

## Step-by-Step Process

### 1. Request for Cancellation (requester)

The process begins with the interested party's petition in the INPI electronic system (e-Marcas), including:

- Identification of the challenged registration (case number)
- Legal grounds
- Proof of legitimate interest
- Payment of the GRU (Federal Collection Guide)

### 2. Notification of the Owner

The INPI publishes the request in the Industrial Property Gazette (RPI) and notifies the owner. The owner has **60 days** to respond, counted from the publication in the RPI.

### 3. Owner's Response (defense)

The owner must present, within the deadline:

- **Evidence of effective use** of the mark in Brazil (sales invoices, registered licensing agreement, advertising material, packaging, point-of-sale photos)
- **Or a legitimate justification for non-use** (import blocked by government restriction, license not granted by a regulatory body, fortuitous event or force majeure)

### 4. INPI Decision (CGREC)

The INPI analyzes the evidence and decides:

- **Unfounded** cancellation — registration remains active
- **Partially founded** — cancellation only for some classes
- **Fully founded** — extinction of registration for all classes

The decision is within the competence of the **General Coordination of Appeals and Administrative Nullity Procedures (CGREC)**, through the **Technical Coordination of Appeals and Administrative Nullity Procedures for Trademarks (COREM)**.

### 5. Appeal

An appeal against the decision may be filed with the **President of the INPI** within 60 days, who decides as the final administrative instance.

## Difference between Cancellation for Non-Use and Nullity

| Aspect | Cancellation for Non-Use | Administrative Nullity |
|--------|--------------------------|------------------------|
| **Ground** | Lack of use | Defect from the outset (conflict, illegality) |
| **Deadline to request** | After 5 years from grant | Up to 180 days from grant (or no deadline for judicial nullity) |
| **INPI code** | 337 | 336 |
| **Cost per class** | BRL 590.00 | BRL 850.00 |
| **Effect** | Extinguishes the registration | Annuls the registration from the beginning |
| **Judging body** | CGREC / COREM | CGREC / COREM |

Administrative nullity (Art. 172 of the LPI) deals with defects in the registration that existed from the start — such as the mark conflicting with a prior right. Cancellation deals with **subsequent non-use**.

## How to Avoid Cancellation

### Best practices for owners

1. **Use the mark effectively** from grant — do not let 5 years pass without commercial activity under the registered name
2. **Keep records of sales invoices** that prove the commercialization of products or services with the mark
3. **Register licensing agreements** with the INPI — use by a qualified licensee also counts as use
4. **Update the specification** with the INPI if the scope of products/services changes
5. **Archive evidence of advertising** (ads, digital campaigns, point-of-sale material)

### What constitutes valid use

The INPI considers as valid use: sales invoices, licensing agreements, dated advertising material, presence at trade fairs with proven dates, packaging with photos, dated e-commerce screenshots, and any other document proving the mark is circulating in the market.

### Legitimate reasons for non-use

Art. 143, §1 allows justifications such as:
- Government restrictions on imports
- Failure to obtain a prior license from a regulatory body
- Fortuitous event or force majeure

However, CGREC case law is strict — mere claims of "economic crisis" or "internal restructuring" are rarely accepted without robust evidence.

## Cancellation in the Context of the Brazilian Trademark System

Cancellation for non-use is one of the instruments that balance the trademark system: the owner receives an exclusive right for 10 years (renewable), but in return must **use the mark**. Without this mechanism, the INPI would become a name reservation office.

In 2026, with the digitization of the e-Marcas system and the more agile work of CGREC, the number of cancellation requests has grown — especially among competitors who find registered but unused marks as obstacles to their own registrations.
