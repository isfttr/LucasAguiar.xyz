Registering a trademark with the INPI is the first step to protect a distinctive sign in Brazil. What many holders do not know is that the registration can be extinguished — even before the end of the 10-year validity period — if the mark is not used. This mechanism is called **cancellation for non-use** and is set forth in Articles 142 to 146 of the Industrial Property Law (Law No. 9.279/96).

This guide covers everything you need to know about trademark cancellation for non-use at the INPI: when it can be requested, by whom, what the cost is, the step-by-step process, how to defend yourself, and how to avoid losing registration due to lack of use.

## What is Trademark Cancellation for Non-Use?

Cancellation for non-use is the extinction of a trademark registration due to lack of use. Unlike nullity (which voids the registration due to a defect in origin, such as a conflict with an earlier mark), cancellation penalizes the holder who **does not use the mark in Brazil** within the legal deadlines.

The legislator's logic is simple: it does not make sense to maintain an active registration that blocks third parties from using similar signs if the holder is not using the mark. The registration exists to protect use in the marketplace, not to reserve signs indefinitely.

> ⚠️ **Important:** Cancellation for non-use does not happen automatically. Someone (individual or legal entity) must **request** it from the INPI. If no one requests it, the registration remains active even without use — but this is not advisable, as any competitor can request it at any time.

## Legal Basis: Articles 142 to 146 of the LPI

Trademark cancellation for non-use is governed by the following articles of Law No. 9.279/96:

| Article | Content |
|--------|----------|
| **Art. 142, III** | The registration is extinguished by cancellation |
| **Art. 143** | The registration will be cancelled if, after 5 years from grant, use has not been initiated in Brazil or has been interrupted for more than 5 consecutive years |
| **Art. 143, §1º** | Cancellation will not occur if the holder justifies non-use for legitimate reasons |
| **Art. 144** | The request for cancellation can be filed by any person with a legitimate interest, upon payment of the fee |
| **Art. 145** | The holder will be notified to respond within **60 days**, under penalty of cancellation |
| **Art. 146** | The holder may present evidence of use or justifications for non-use |

The administrative regulation of the procedure is in **INPI/PR Resolution No. 249/2018** and **INPI/PR Ordinance No. 79/2022**, which establish the procedural rules.

## Deadlines and Requirements

### 5-year period from grant

The request for cancellation can only be filed after **5 years from the date of registration grant**. The starting point is the date of issuance of the registration certificate, not the filing date of the application.

Example: a mark granted in March 2021 can only have its registration challenged for cancellation as of March 2026.

### When cancellation can be requested

There are two situations provided for in Art. 143:

| Situation | Description |
|----------|-----------|
| **Non-initiation of use** | After 5 years from grant, the holder has not started using the mark in Brazil |
| **Interruption of use** | The holder used the mark, but interrupted use for more than 5 consecutive years |

### Who can request

Any person with a **legitimate interest** can file the request. In practice, the requester is almost always a competitor or third party interested in registering a similar sign that finds the mark as an obstacle in the INPI examination.

The INPI understands that interest is presumed when the requester has a trademark application rejected based on the questioned mark, or when they demonstrate activity in the same field.

### Use that does not prevent cancellation

The INPI considers as **effective use** the use of the mark as registered, on products or services actually placed on the market. The following are not considered valid use:

- Merely symbolic use (to "hold" the registration)
- Use only in internal materials (letterhead, invoices) without commercialization
- Use on an insignificant scale without real commercial activity
- Use on products or services different from those specified in the registration

## Step-by-Step Process

### 1. Request for Cancellation (requester)

The process begins with the interested party's petition in the INPI electronic system (e-Marcas), including:

- Identification of the questioned registration (process number)
- Legal grounds
- Proof of legitimate interest
- Payment of the GRU (Federal Collection Guide)

### 2. Notification of the Holder

The INPI publishes the request in the Industrial Property Journal (RPI) and notifies the holder. The holder has **60 days** to respond, counted from the publication in the RPI.

### 3. Holder's Response (defense)

The holder must present, within the deadline:

- **Evidence of effective use** of the mark in Brazil (sales invoices, recorded licensing agreement, advertising material, packaging, point-of-sale photos)
- **Or a legitimate justification for non-use** (import prevented by government restriction, license not granted by a regulatory body, fortuitous event or force majeure)

### 4. INPI Decision (CGREC)

The INPI analyzes the evidence and decides for:

- **Dismissal** of cancellation — the registration remains active
- **Partial grant** — cancellation only for some classes
- **Full grant** — extinction of the registration for all classes

The decision is under the responsibility of the **General Coordination of Appeals and Administrative Nullity Proceedings (CGREC)**, through the **Technical Coordination of Appeals and Administrative Nullity Proceedings for Trademarks (COREM)**.

### 5. Appeal

An appeal against the decision may be filed with the **President of the INPI** within 60 days, who decides at the last administrative instance.

## Difference Between Cancellation and Nullity

| Aspect | Cancellation | Administrative Nullity |
|---------|-----------|------------------------|
| **Ground** | Lack of use | Defect in origin (conflict, illegality) |
| **Deadline to request** | After 5 years from grant | Up to 180 days from grant (or no deadline for judicial nullity) |
| **INPI code** | 337 | 336 |
| **Cost per class** | R$ 590.00 | R$ 850.00 |
| **Effect** | Extinguishes the registration | Annuls the registration from the start |
| **Who judges** | CGREC / COREM | CGREC / COREM |

Administrative nullity (Art. 172 of the LPI) deals with defects in the registration that existed from the beginning — such as the mark conflicting with an earlier right. Cancellation deals with **subsequent non-use**.

## How to Avoid Cancellation

### Best practices for holders

1. **Use the mark effectively** from the grant — do not let 5 years pass without commercial activity under the registered name
2. **Keep records of sales invoices** that prove the commercialization of products or services with the mark
3. **Register licensing agreements** with the INPI — use by a qualified licensee also counts as use
4. **Update the specification** with the INPI if the scope of products/services changes
5. **Archive evidence of advertising** (ads, digital campaigns, point-of-sale materials)

### What constitutes valid use

The INPI considers valid use: sales invoices, licensing agreements, dated advertising material, presence at trade fairs with confirmed dates, product packaging with photos, dated e-commerce screenshots, and any other document proving that the mark is circulating in the market.

### Legitimate reasons for non-use

Art. 143, §1º allows justifications such as:
- Government restrictions on imports
- Need for prior license from a regulatory body not obtained
- Fortuitous event or force majeure

The CGREC case law, however, is strict — mere allegations of "economic crisis" or "internal restructuring" are rarely accepted without robust evidence.

## Cancellation in the Context of the Brazilian Trademark System

Cancellation for non-use is one of the instruments that balance the trademark system: the holder receives an exclusive right for 10 years (renewable), but in return must **use the mark**. Without this mechanism, the INPI would become a name-reservation office.

In 2026, with the digitization of the e-Marcas system and the more agile performance of the CGREC, the number of cancellation requests has grown — especially among competitors who find registered but unused marks as obstacles to their own registrations.
