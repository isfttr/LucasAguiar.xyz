On July 5, 2026, the Brazilian Intellectual Property Association (ABPI) announced the launch of its **1st Academic Papers and Abstracts Competition**, a brand-new initiative to identify and showcase research, studies, and practical cases that contribute to the advancement of Intellectual Property, innovation, technology, and business in Brazil.

Submissions are open until **July 22, 2026** — a concrete opportunity for researchers, professionals, and students to present their work to a qualified audience of decision-makers in the Brazilian innovation ecosystem.

## What the Competition Is About

The competition covers the following themes:

- **Intellectual Property** (patents, trademarks, industrial designs, geographical indications, copyright)
- **Innovation and technology**
- **Business and digital transformation**
- **Practical cases of strategic IP use**

Selected works will be presented at the **Paper, Abstract, and Case Study Session** of the ABPI Global Innovation and Intellectual Property Meeting, taking place **August 16–18**, in Rio de Janeiro.

## Who Can Apply

| Category | Target Audience |
|----------|----------------|
| **Academia** | Professors, researchers, master's and PhD students |
| **IP Professionals** | Lawyers, patent agents, consultants |
| **Companies & Startups** | Innovative firms with IP case studies |
| **ICTs & NITs** | Innovation hubs and technology transfer offices |
| **International** | Foreign researchers and professionals |

## Benefits for Selected Participants

Selected participants receive:

- **Institutional recognition certificate** from ABPI
- **Registration for ABPI Congress 2027** (where applicable)
- **One-year ABPI membership** (where applicable)
- **Potential publication in Revista da ABPI**, subject to editorial review
- **Institutional visibility** across ABPI's official channels and event materials
- **Cash prizes**

## Context: ABPI and the Brazilian IP Ecosystem

The academic competition is part of a broader ABPI effort to strengthen Brazil's IP system. In June 2026, ABPI secured a [landmark victory at the TRF-2]({{< relref "posts/trf2-decisao-abpi-fortalecimento-inpi/" >}}), ordering the restructuring of Brazil's Patent Office (INPI). ABPI also plays an active role in the National IP Strategy (ENPI) and the Interministerial IP Group (GIPI).

Brazil faces a historical shortage of IP specialists. With the modernization of INPI, the growth of patent filings (especially in [semiconductors and digital technologies]({{< relref "posts/mapa-global-patentes-pct-2025-brasil/" >}})), and the ongoing ENPI implementation, the demand for qualified IP professionals is set to rise significantly.

This competition helps:
1. Develop the next generation of IP researchers and professionals in Brazil
2. Produce applied knowledge bridging academia and market
3. Give visibility to research that would otherwise remain within academic circles
4. Strengthen the Brazilian IP community

## How to Apply

Submissions are made through the [ABPI website](https://abpi.org.br/), where the full rules, selection criteria, and prize details are available.
