The Brazilian intellectual property system offers different protection modalities, each with specific requirements, deadlines, and costs. Choosing the wrong type can mean losing the right to protect your creation. This guide compares all modalities available in Brazil, based on the Industrial Property Law (LPI — Law 9,279/96), the Copyright Law (Law 9,610/98), the Plant Variety Protection Law (Law 9,456/97), and INPI’s normative acts.

## Overview of modalities

| Modality | Legal basis | Body | Term of protection | Approximate cost (INPI fees 2026) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Invention patent (PI) | LPI (Law 9,279/96) | INPI | 20 years from filing date | BRL 130–2,500 (depending on size) |
| Utility model (MU) | LPI (Law 9,279/96) | INPI | 15 years from filing date | BRL 110–1,900 |
| Industrial design (DI) | LPI (Law 9,279/96) | INPI | 10 years + 3 renewals of 5 years (max. 25 years) | BRL 115–1,700 |
| Trademark | LPI (Law 9,279/96) | INPI | 10 years, renewable for equal periods | BRL 365–2,800 (per class) |
| Geographical indication (GI) | LPI (Law 9,279/96) | INPI | Indefinite (as long as conditions are maintained) | BRL 1,200–4,500 |
| Computer program | Law 9,609/98 + Law 9,610/98 | INPI (registration) | 50 years from creation or January 1 of the following year | BRL 85–1,400 |
| Plant variety | Law 9,456/97 (Plant Variety Protection Law) | SNPC/MAPA | 15 years (18 for vines, trees) | Variable (SNPC fees) |

> **Note:** Cost ranges reflect official INPI and SNPC fees in 2026 for individuals and MEI (lowest value) up to large legal entities (highest value). Check the [INPI fee table](https://www.gov.br/inpi/pt-br/servicos/tabela-de-retribuicoes) for exact amounts.

## Invention patent (PI)

Protects an **invention** — the solution to a technical problem that meets the requirements of novelty, inventive step, and industrial applicability.

- **What it covers:** products, processes, compositions, technical methods
- **Requirements:** worldwide novelty, inventive step, industrial applicability
- **Term:** 20 years from filing date
- **Examination:** required within 36 months of filing; average backlog of 5–8 years at INPI
- **Prior art search:** recommended before filing ([accreditation guide for searches]({{< relref "posts/inpi-credenciamento-busca-anterioridade-patentes/" >}}))

## Utility model (MU)

Protects a **functional improvement** in objects of practical use — the "new shape" that improves the usability or manufacture of something already existing.

- **What it covers:** physical objects with a new form or arrangement resulting in functional improvement (e.g., new tool, functional packaging)
- **Requirements:** novelty, inventive step (less strict than PI), industrial applicability
- **Term:** 15 years from filing date
- **Key difference from PI:** protects form/functionality, not processes or chemical compositions; lower inventive step requirement

## Industrial design (DI)

Protects the **ornamental appearance** of an object — the set of lines, shapes, colors, and textures that give it a distinctive visual aspect.

- **What it covers:** three‑dimensional visual configuration or two‑dimensional ornamental pattern applied to a product
- **Requirements:** novelty and originality (no inventive step required)
- **Term:** 10 years from filing date, renewable for 3 periods of 5 years (max. 25 years)
- **Process:** faster registration than patents (average 6–12 months at INPI)
- See the [complete industrial design registration guide]({{< relref "posts/guia-registro-desenho-industrial-inpi-2026/" >}})

## Trademark

Protects the **distinctive sign** that identifies a company’s products or services and distinguishes them from competitors.

- **What it covers:** word marks, figurative marks, combined marks, three‑dimensional marks, position marks, and sound marks (since 2024)
- **Requirements:** distinctiveness, lawfulness, no conflict with prior marks
- **Term:** 10 years, indefinitely renewable
- **Classification:** International Classification of Nice (45 classes)
- **Cost:** BRL 365 (MEI/individual) to BRL 2,800 (large entity) per class
- See the [trademark registration cost guide]({{< relref "posts/quanto-custa-registrar-marca-inpi-2026/" >}})

## Geographical indication (GI)

Protects the **link between product and territory** — when the product’s qualities, reputation, or characteristics are essentially due to its geographical origin.

- **Types:**
  - **Indication of provenance (IP):** geographical name known for production/extraction of the good
  - **Denomination of origin (DO):** qualities exclusively or essentially attributable to the geographical environment (natural and human factors)
- **Term:** indefinite (as long as conditions are maintained)
- **Who may apply:** associations, unions, representative institutes
- See the [complete geographical indications guide]({{< relref "posts/guia-indicacoes-geograficas-inpi-brasil/" >}})

## Computer program (software)

Software in Brazil is protected under **copyright** (Law 9,609/98 and Law 9,610/98). Registration at INPI is declaratory and facilitates proof of authorship, but does not constitute the right.

- **What it covers:** source code, object code, technical documentation
- **Requirements:** originality (no novelty or inventive step required)
- **Term:** 50 years counted from January 1 of the year following creation/publication
- **Registration:** optional, done at INPI via a specific system (recently reactivated — July 2026)
- See the [computer program registration guide]({{< relref "posts/guia-registro-programa-computador-inpi/" >}})

## Plant variety

Protects **plant varieties** developed through genetic improvement, under the Plant Variety Protection Law (Law 9,456/97), with the National Plant Variety Protection Service (SNPC/MAPA).

- **What it covers:** new plant varieties of any genus or species
- **Requirements:** distinctness, uniformity, stability, commercial novelty
- **Term:** 15 years (18 for vines, trees, and forest species)
- **Difference from biotech patent:** plant variety protects the plant variety itself; a patent protects the process or genetically modified molecule

## How to choose the right type

The choice depends on the **object of creation** and the **business objective**:

1. **Is it a technical solution to a problem?** → Invention patent or utility model
   - Is the solution a process, composition, or radically new product? → **Invention patent**
   - Is it a functional improvement on an existing object? → **Utility model**

2. **Is it the visual appearance of a product?** → Industrial design

3. **Is it a sign to identify your product/service in the market?** → Trademark
   - Is the sign a geographical name and does the product have a reputation linked to the territory? → **Geographical indication**

4. **Is it software?** → Copyright + optional registration at INPI

5. **Is it a new plant variety?** → Plant variety (SNPC)

When in doubt, you can file more than one application for the same product — for example, a bottle can have an invention patent (sealing mechanism), utility model (functional cap), industrial design (ornamental shape), and trademark (name).

## Procedural timelines at INPI

Processing time varies greatly among modalities:

| Modality | Filing → Publication | Publication → Decision | Total approximate |
|---|---|---|---|
| Invention patent | 6–18 months | 5–7 years | 5–8 years |
| Utility model | 4–8 months | 3–5 years | 3–5 years |
| Industrial design | 2–6 months | 4–10 months | 6–16 months |
| Trademark | 1–3 months | 6–18 months | 8–24 months |
| Geographical indication | 3–6 months | 12–24 months | 18–36 months |
| Computer program | Immediate | 30–60 days | 30–60 days |
