The Brazilian intellectual property system offers different protection modalities, each with specific requirements, terms and costs. Choosing the wrong type can mean losing the right to protect your creation. This guide compares all modalities available in Brazil, based on the Industrial Property Law (LPI — Law 9.279/96), the Copyright Law (Law 9.610/98), the Plant Variety Protection Law (Law 9.456/97), and INPI's normative acts.

## Overview of modalities

| Modality | Legal basis | Authority | Protection term | Approximate cost (INPI fees 2026) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Invention patent (PI) | LPI (Law 9.279/96) | INPI | 20 years from filing date | R$ 130–2,500 (depending on entity size) |
| Utility model (MU) | LPI (Law 9.279/96) | INPI | 15 years from filing date | R$ 110–1,900 |
| Industrial design (DI) | LPI (Law 9.279/96) | INPI | 10 years + 3 renewals of 5 years (max. 25 years) | R$ 115–1,700 |
| Trademark | LPI (Law 9.279/96) | INPI | 10 years, renewable indefinitely | R$ 365–2,800 (per class) |
| Geographical indication (GI) | LPI (Law 9.279/96) | INPI | Indefinite (while conditions are maintained) | R$ 1,200–4,500 |
| Computer program (software) | Law 9.609/98 + Law 9.610/98 | INPI (registration) | 50 years from creation or January 1 of following year | R$ 85–1,400 |
| Plant variety | Law 9.456/97 (Cultivar Law) | SNPC/MAPA | 15 years (18 for vines, trees) | Variable (SNPC fees) |

> **Note:** The cost ranges reflect official INPI and SNPC fees in 2026 for individuals and MEI (lowest value) up to large corporations (highest value). Check the [INPI fee table](https://www.gov.br/inpi/pt-br/servicos/tabela-de-retribuicoes) for exact amounts.

## Invention patent (PI)

Protects an **invention** — the solution to a technical problem that meets the requirements of novelty, inventive step, and industrial applicability.

- **Coverage:** products, processes, compositions, technical methods
- **Requirements:** worldwide novelty, inventive step, industrial applicability
- **Term:** 20 years from filing date
- **Examination:** must be requested within 36 months of filing; average backlog of 5–8 years at INPI
- **Prior art search:** recommended before filing ([credentialing guide for searches]({{< relref "posts/inpi-credenciamento-busca-anterioridade-patentes/" >}}))

## Utility model (MU)

Protects a **functional improvement** in objects of practical use — a new shape or arrangement that improves the usability or manufacturing of an existing object.

- **Coverage:** physical objects with new shape or arrangement resulting in functional improvement
- **Requirements:** novelty, inventive act (lower threshold than PI), industrial applicability
- **Term:** 15 years from filing date
- **Key difference from PI:** protects shape/functionality, not processes or chemical compositions; lower inventive step requirement

## Industrial design (DI)

Protects the **ornamental appearance** of an object — the set of lines, shapes, colors and textures that give it a distinctive visual aspect.

- **Coverage:** three-dimensional visual configuration or two-dimensional ornamental pattern applied to a product
- **Requirements:** novelty and originality (no inventive step required)
- **Term:** 10 years from filing date, renewable for 3 periods of 5 years (max. 25 years)
- **Process:** faster registration than patents (average 6–12 months at INPI)
- See the [complete industrial design registration guide]({{< relref "posts/guia-registro-desenho-industrial-inpi-2026/" >}})

## Trademark

Protects the **distinctive sign** that identifies a company's products or services and distinguishes them from competitors.

- **Coverage:** word marks, figurative marks, mixed marks, three-dimensional marks, position marks, and sound marks (since 2024)
- **Requirements:** distinctiveness, lawfulness, no conflict with prior marks
- **Term:** 10 years, renewable indefinitely
- **Classification:** International Nice Classification (45 classes)
- **Cost:** R$ 365 (MEI/individual) to R$ 2,800 (large entity) per class
- See the [trademark registration cost guide]({{< relref "posts/quanto-custa-registrar-marca-inpi-2026/" >}})

## Geographical indication (GI)

Protects the **link between product and territory** — when qualities, reputation or characteristics of a product are essentially due to its geographical origin.

- **Types:**
  - **Indication of source (IP):** geographical name known for producing/extracting the good
  - **Denomination of origin (DO):** qualities exclusively or essentially attributable to the geographical environment (natural and human factors)
- **Term:** indefinite (while conditions are maintained)
- **Who can apply:** associations, unions, representative institutes
- See the [complete geographical indications guide]({{< relref "posts/guia-indicacoes-geograficas-inpi-brasil/" >}})

## Computer program (software)

Software in Brazil is protected under **copyright** (Law 9.609/98 and Law 9.610/98). Registration with INPI is declaratory and facilitates proof of authorship, but is not constitutive of the right.

- **Coverage:** source code, object code, technical documentation
- **Requirements:** originality (novelty or inventive step not required)
- **Term:** 50 years counted from January 1 of the year following creation/publication
- **Registration:** optional, done at INPI via a specific system (recently reactivated — July 2026)
- See the [software registration guide]({{< relref "posts/guia-registro-programa-computador-inpi/" >}})

## Plant variety

Protects **plant varieties** developed through genetic improvement, under the Plant Variety Protection Law (Law 9.456/97), through the National Plant Variety Protection Service (SNPC/MAPA).

- **Coverage:** new plant varieties of any genus or species
- **Requirements:** distinctness, uniformity, stability, commercial novelty
- **Term:** 15 years (18 for vines, trees, and forest species)
- **Difference from biotech patent:** plant variety protects the plant variety itself; a patent protects the process or genetically modified molecule

## How to choose the right type

The choice depends on the **subject matter of the creation** and the **business objective**:

1. **Is it a technical solution to a problem?** → Invention patent or utility model
   - Is the solution a process, composition or radically new product? → **Invention patent**
   - Is it a functional improvement to an existing object? → **Utility model**

2. **Is it the visual appearance of a product?** → Industrial design

3. **Is it a sign to identify your product/service in the market?** → Trademark
   - Is the sign a geographical name and does the product have a reputation linked to the territory? → **Geographical indication**

4. **Is it software?** → Copyright + optional INPI registration

5. **Is it a new plant variety?** → Plant variety (SNPC)

When in doubt, multiple applications for the same product are possible — for example, a bottle can have an invention patent (sealing mechanism), utility model (functional cap), industrial design (ornamental shape), and trademark (name).

## Processing times at INPI

Processing time varies greatly between modalities:

| Modality | Filing → Publication | Publication → Decision | Total approximate |
|---|---|---|---|
| Invention patent | 6–18 months | 5–7 years | 5–8 years |
| Utility model | 4–8 months | 3–5 years | 3–5 years |
| Industrial design | 2–6 months | 4–10 months | 6–16 months |
| Trademark | 1–3 months | 6–18 months | 8–24 months |
| Geographical indication | 3–6 months | 12–24 months | 18–36 months |
| Computer program | Immediate | 30–60 days | 30–60 days |

## Applicable legislation

- **LPI (Law 9.279/96):** patents, trademarks, industrial designs, GIs, unfair competition — [full text (Portuguese)](https://www.planalto.gov.br/ccivil_03/leis/l9279.htm)
- **Software Law (Law 9.609/98):** computer program protection
- **Copyright Law (Law 9.610/98):** copyright basis for software
- **Plant Variety Law (Law 9.456/97):** protection of new plant varieties
- **Budapest Treaty:** international deposit of microorganisms (Decree 13,011/2026) — see our [complete guide]({{< relref "posts/brasil-tratado-budapeste-patentes-biotecnologia/" >}})
- **Madrid Protocol:** international trademark registration
