The Industrial Design (ID) registration protects the ornamental appearance of an object — the three-dimensional shape of a package, a piece of furniture, a toy, or the two-dimensional aspects such as a fabric print or the decorative pattern of a ceramic piece. Unlike a patent (which protects technical function) or a trademark (which protects commercial identity), the ID protects the product's **visual design**.

In Brazil, registration is granted by the INPI and regulated by the Industrial Property Law (Law No. 9.279/96), articles 95 to 106. This guide covers the entire process: what can and cannot be registered, costs, documents, step-by-step electronic filing, deadlines, and the international route via the Hague Agreement.

> **Updated July 2026:** On 06/26/2026, the INPI published the latest update to the Industrial Designs Manual, with changes to document copies, proof of union priority, and compliance petitions. The e-DI system continues normal operation.

## What is an Industrial Design?

**Article 95 of the LPI** defines industrial design as "the ornamental plastic form of an object or the ornamental set of lines and colors that can be applied to a product, providing a new and original visual result in its external configuration and that can serve as a type of industrial manufacture."

In practice, ID protects:

| What it registers | Examples |
|----------------|----------|
| **Three-dimensional form** | Design of a bottle, chair, smartphone, jewelry, lamp |
| **Two-dimensional pattern** | Fabric print, wallpaper pattern, graphic applied to a surface |
| **Ornamental set** | Combination of form + pattern in the same product |
| **Part of a product** | Handbag strap, base of an appliance (as long as visible and ornamental) |

**What CANNOT be registered as ID:**

- Purely artistic works (sculptures, paintings — protected by copyright)
- Technical or functional aspects (protected by invention patent or utility model)
- Distinctive signs (protected as a trademark)
- Engineering works or architectural projects
- Shapes necessary for a technical effect (e.g., mandatory aerodynamic shape)

> ⚠️ **Important:** if the shape of a product is determined exclusively by its technical function, it **cannot** be registered as an industrial design. This prevents so-called "evergreening" of functional designs.

## Industrial Design vs. Other Protections

It is common to confuse the types of protection. The table below compares the main differences:

| Aspect | Industrial Design | Invention Patent | Utility Model | Trademark |
|---------|-------------------|--------------------|--------------------|-------|
| **What it protects** | Ornamental appearance | New and inventive invention | Functional improvement | Commercial identity |
| **Substantive examination** | Yes (novelty + originality) | Yes (inventive step) | Yes (inventive step) | Yes (distinctiveness) |
| **Validity term** | 10 years + 3 renewals of 5 years = **25 years** | 20 years | 15 years | 10 years (renewable indefinitely) |
| **Novelty examination** | Worldwide (state of the art) | Worldwide | Worldwide | National (conflict) |
| **Average grant time** | 6-12 months | 5-10 years (with backlog) | 3-5 years | 12-18 months |
| **Publication** | Automatic (18 months) | Automatic (18 months) | Automatic (18 months) | After formal examination |

ID is generally the **fastest and cheapest** way to protect the appearance of an industrialized product.

## Legal Requirements: Novelty and Originality

To be registered, the industrial design must meet two requirements:

**1. Novelty (Art. 96 LPI):** the design cannot be comprised in the state of the art. The state of the art is everything made accessible to the public before the filing date, in Brazil or abroad, by written or oral description, use, or any other means.

**2. Originality (Art. 97 LPI):** the design must result in a distinctive visual configuration compared to prior objects. Inventive step (as in a patent) is not required, but the visual result must not be merely trivial or arising from obvious combinations of known elements.

### Grace Period (Art. 96, §2 LPI)

The inventor may disclose their design **up to 180 days before filing** without losing novelty, provided the disclosure was made by the inventor themselves, by third parties based on the inventor's information, or by the INPI in an official publication. This period is important for fairs, exhibitions, and commercial presentations.

## Required Documents

To file an ID registration application with the INPI, you need:

1. **Reproductions of the industrial design** — figures or photographs showing the object from all necessary angles (perspective, front, side, top, bottom). The Industrial Designs Manual (item 5.5) details the presentation rules:
   - Format: JPEG or PNG
   - Minimum resolution: 300 DPI
   - Neutral background (preferably white)
   - Hatching lines to indicate transparency or materials
2. **Descriptive report** (optional for ID, but recommended) — describes what each view represents
3. **Power of attorney** — if filed by a proxy/attorney
4. **Proof of payment of the GRU** — with the number generated in the e-INPI system
5. **Priority declaration** — if claiming union priority (6-month term from the foreign filing)

> **Three-dimensional vs. two-dimensional designs:** the INPI treats them differently. Three-dimensional designs require multiple views (usually 6: perspective, front, back, left side, right side, top, bottom). Two-dimensional designs (prints) may require only one front view, with indication of rapport (repetition) when applicable.

## Step-by-Step Registration

### Step 1: Registration in e-INPI

Before any procedure, you need to register at [e-INPI](https://www.gov.br/inpi/pt-br/servicos/e-inpi), the INPI's electronic services system. Individuals use a gov.br account (silver or gold level). Legal entities need an ICP-Brazil digital certificate of the legal representative.

See the [guide to accessing CNPJ with a gov.br account]({{< relref "posts/inpi-acesso-cnpj-conta-govbr-guia-2026/" >}}) for more details.

### Step 2: Issue and pay the GRU

Access the [GRU Issuance System](https://www.gov.br/inpi/pt-br/servicos/desenhos-industriais/custos-e-pagamento) and issue the Federal Collection Guide for the desired fee code (filing = 300, examination = 301, etc.).

- Payment is made via barcode at any bank
- Keep the GRU number — it will be needed for filing
- The GRU expires in 30 days (do not confuse with the legal deadlines of the process)

### Step 3: File the application in e-DI

After paying the GRU, access the [e-DI system](https://www.gov.br/inpi/pt-br/servicos/desenhos-industriais) and:

1. Select "New ID Registration Application"
2. Enter the paid GRU number
3. Fill in the applicant's and author's data (they may be different persons)
4. Upload the design figures/photographs
5. If claiming union priority, attach the priority document
6. Confirm and submit

### Step 4: Formal examination

The INPI carries out a formal examination to verify that the documentation is complete and correct. If there are irregularities, the INPI publishes a **requirement** in the RPI — you have 60 days (extendable by another 60) to comply.

### Step 5: Substantive examination

The INPI examines novelty and originality based on the worldwide state of the art. If approved, the registration is granted and published in the RPI for third-party knowledge.

### Step 6: Certificate

After approval and payment of the certificate fee (code 310), the INPI issues the industrial design registration certificate.

**Estimated total timeline:** 6 to 18 months, significantly faster than patents (which may take 5-10 years).

## Validity Term and Maintenance

The ID registration has an initial term of **10 years** counted from the filing date, renewable for **three successive 5-year periods**, totaling up to **25 years** of protection.

| Period | Action | Fee |
|---------|------|------|
| Years 1-5 | Initial maintenance fee (already included in filing) | — |
| Year 5 | Quinquennial maintenance/renewal | Code 305 |
| Year 10 | 1st renewal for 5 years | Code 305 |
| Year 15 | 2nd renewal for 5 years | Code 305 |
| Year 20 | 3rd renewal for 5 years | Code 305 |

> ⚠️ **The maintenance fee (code 305) must be paid every 5 years, counted from the filing.** Non-payment implies extinction of the registration. The INPI sends reminders through the "My Applications" system.

## Hague Agreement: International Registration

Brazil joined the **Hague Agreement for the International Registration of Industrial Designs** in 2023, administered by WIPO. This allows a single international application to protect the design in up to **96 countries** (all contracting parties of the Agreement).

### Advantages of the Hague Agreement

- **Single application** in one language (English, French, or Spanish)
- **Unified fees** in Swiss francs (CHF)
- Up to **100 designs** per application (same Locarno class)
- **Minimum 15 years** of international protection (renewable up to the maximum term of the designated country)
- In Brazil, the term via Hague also reaches **25 years**

### How to file via Hague

1. Access [eHague](https://www.wipo.int/hague/en/eHague/) — WIPO's online platform
2. Fill out the form and upload the reproductions
3. Designate the countries where you seek protection (include Brazil if you want national protection)
4. Pay the fees: basic + publication + designation per country
5. WIPO examines formal requirements and publishes in the International Designs Bulletin

Each designated country may carry out its own substantive examination — the INPI has 6 months to refuse protection in Brazil based on national legislation.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Do I need a lawyer to register?

It is not mandatory for individuals or legal entities. The e-DI system is self-instructional. However, an IP agent can help with drafting the descriptive report and the international protection strategy. If you are abroad without domicile in Brazil, you need to appoint a proxy in the country.

### Can I register a design I already sell?

Yes, provided the sale took place **less than 180 days ago** (grace period under Art. 96, §2 of the LPI). After that period, the design loses novelty.

### Does the ID registration cover the entire product or just the design?

Only the appearance. If a competitor copies your packaging but uses a different shape, there may be no infringement. Therefore, for innovative products, ID + patent (for function) + trademark (for identity) are combined.

### What is the difference between ID and Copyright?

ID protects the design applied to industry (object produced at scale). Copyright protects the work as an aesthetic creation, independent of industrial application. In Brazil, cumulative protection is possible: the design of a piece of jewelry can be registered as ID and also protected by copyright.

### Does ID cover digital designs (UI/UX)?

User interface (GUI) can be protected as ID in Brazil if applied to an industrial product — for example, the visual layout of an application when displayed on a smartphone screen. The INPI has already granted ID registrations for GUIs, provided industrial application is demonstrated.

## What changed in the Industrial Designs Manual (June 2026)

On June 26, 2026, the INPI published the latest update to the ID Manual. The main changes:

1. **Document copies:** clearer guidance on how to request copies of ID process documents, with a standardized procedure
2. **Proof of union priority obtained by assignment:** new documentary requirements when the priority right is acquired by assignment from third parties
3. **Compliance petitions:** adjustments to forms for responding to INPI requirements

The update is specific but important for those operating with union priority (filing abroad followed by filing in Brazil within 6 months).

## Conclusion

Industrial design registration is a strategic and underutilized tool in Brazil. With a much faster grant timeline than patents, affordable costs (especially with the 70% discount for individuals and SMEs), and the possibility of international protection via the Hague Agreement, ID should be on the radar of any company investing in product design.

The INPI's ID Manual update in June 2026 shows that the agency is adjusting its procedures to keep up with the growing volume of applications — driven by Brazil's accession to the Hague Agreement in 2023 and increased awareness of the importance of industrial design as an intellectual property asset.
