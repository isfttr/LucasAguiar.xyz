Registering a trademark with Brazil's INPI is the first step to protecting a distinctive sign in the country. What many trademark owners don't know is that the registration can be extinguished — even before its 10-year term ends — if the mark is not used. This mechanism is called **caducidade** (cancellation for non-use) and is governed by Articles 142 to 146 of Brazil's Industrial Property Law (Law No. 9,279/96).

This guide covers everything you need to know about trademark cancellation for non-use before the INPI: when it can be requested, by whom, costs, step-by-step procedure, how to defend against it, and how to keep your registration active.

## What Is Trademark Cancellation for Non-Use?

Trademark cancellation for non-use (caducidade) is the extinction of a trademark registration for lack of use. Unlike administrative nullity (which voids a registration due to an inherent defect, such as conflict with an earlier mark), caducidade penalizes the owner who **does not use the mark in Brazil** within the legal timeframes.

The logic is straightforward: it makes no sense to keep an active registration blocking third parties from using similar signs if the owner is not using the mark. The registration exists to protect market use, not to reserve signs indefinitely.

> ⚠️ **Important:** Cancellation does not happen automatically. Someone (individual or legal entity) must **file a request** with the INPI. If nobody requests it, the registration remains active even without use — but any competitor can file at any time.

## Legal Basis: Articles 142 to 146 of the Brazilian IP Law

| Article | Content |
|---------|---------|
| **Art. 142, III** | The registration is extinguished by cancellation for non-use |
| **Art. 143** | Registration shall be cancelled if, 5 years after grant, use has not started in Brazil or has been interrupted for more than 5 consecutive years |
| **Art. 143, §1** | Cancellation shall not occur if the owner justifies non-use on legitimate grounds |
| **Art. 144** | Any person with a legitimate interest may request cancellation, upon payment of the prescribed fee |
| **Art. 145** | The owner shall be notified to respond within **60 days**, failing which cancellation is declared |
| **Art. 146** | The owner may present proof of use or justifications for non-use |

The administrative procedure is regulated by **INPI Resolution No. 249/2018** and **INPI Ordinance No. 79/2022**.

## Timeframes and Requirements

### 5-Year Window from Grant

A cancellation request can only be filed **5 years after the registration grant date**. The starting point is the date of registration certification, not the filing date.

Example: a mark granted in March 2021 can only be challenged for non-use from March 2026 onward.

### Grounds for Cancellation

| Scenario | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Failure to initiate use** | 5 years after grant, the owner has not started using the mark in Brazil |
| **Interruption of use** | The owner used the mark but suspended use for more than 5 consecutive years |

### Who May Request

Any person with a **legitimate interest** may file. In practice, the requester is almost always a competitor or third party whose trademark application was rejected due to an earlier, unused registration.

The INPI considers interest to be presumed when the requester has a trademark application rejected based on the challenged mark, or when they demonstrate activity in the same industry.

### What Qualifies as Use

The INPI considers **effective use** as actual commercialization of products or services under the mark as registered. The following do NOT qualify:

- Merely token use (just to "hold" the registration)
- Internal use only (letterhead, invoices) without market presence
- Insignificant commercial activity
- Use for products/services different from those specified in the registration

## Step-by-Step Procedure

### 1. Filing the Request (Claimant)

The process starts with a petition through the INPI's electronic system (e-Marcas), containing:
- Identification of the challenged registration (case number)
- Legal grounds
- Proof of legitimate interest
- Payment receipt (GRU)

### 2. Notification of the Owner

The INPI publishes the request in the Industrial Property Gazette (RPI) and notifies the owner. The owner has **60 days** to respond, counted from the RPI publication date.

### 3. Owner's Response (Defense)

The owner must submit, within the deadline:
- **Proof of effective use** in Brazil (invoices, registered license agreements, dated advertising materials, packaging, point-of-sale photos)
- **Or legitimate justification** for non-use (government import restrictions, pending regulatory license, force majeure)

### 4. INPI Decision (CGREC)

The INPI analyzes the evidence and decides:
- **Unfounded** — registration remains active
- **Partial** — cancellation applies only to certain classes
- **Total** — registration extinguished for all classes

Decisions are made by the **Coordination-General of Appeals and Administrative Nullity Proceedings (CGREC)**, through the **Technical Coordination of Appeals and Nullity of Trademarks (COREM)**.

### 5. Appeal

The decision may be appealed to the **President of the INPI** within 60 days, whose ruling is final at the administrative level.

## Cancellation vs. Administrative Nullity

| Aspect | Cancellation (Caducidade) | Administrative Nullity |
|--------|--------------------------|----------------------|
| **Ground** | Lack of use | Inherent defect (conflict, illegality) |
| **Deadline to file** | After 5 years from grant | Within 180 days of grant (or unlimited for judicial nullity) |
| **INPI Code** | 337 | 336 |
| **Cost per class** | R$ 590.00 | R$ 850.00 |
| **Effect** | Extinguishes from filing date | Voids registration from origin |
| **Decided by** | CGREC / COREM | CGREC / COREM |

Administrative nullity (Art. 172 of the IP Law) addresses defects that existed from the start — such as the mark conflicting with a prior right. Cancellation addresses **supervening lack of use**.

## How to Avoid Trademark Cancellation

### Best Practices for Owners

1. **Use the mark effectively** from grant — don't let 5 years pass without market activity
2. **Keep invoice records** showing commercialization under the mark
3. **Register license agreements** with the INPI — use by a qualified licensee counts as use
4. **Update the specification** if your product/service scope changes
5. **Archive advertising proof** (campaigns, digital ads, point-of-sale materials)

### Valid Use Evidence

The INPI accepts as valid proof: sales invoices, registered license agreements, dated advertising materials, trade show participation records, packaging photos, dated e-commerce screenshots, and any document showing the mark circulating in the market.

### Legitimate Grounds for Non-Use

Article 143, §1 allows justifications such as:
- Government import restrictions
- Pending regulatory license
- Force majeure

The CGREC's jurisprudence, however, is strict — mere claims of "economic crisis" or "internal restructuring" are rarely accepted without robust evidence.
