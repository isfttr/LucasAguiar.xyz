The National Institute of Industrial Property (INPI) published **Public Call No. 01/2026** to select successful cases related to the strategic use of intellectual property assets. The initiative aims to form the **INPI's Bank of Intellectual Property Success Stories**, gathering experiences that demonstrate how IP can contribute to innovation, competitiveness, economic development, and social impact in Brazil.

The official news was published on the INPI website ([link para a notícia](https://www.gov.br/inpi/pt-br/central-de-conteudo/noticias/inpi-lanca-edital-para-selecionar-casos-de-sucesso-em-propriedade-intelectual)), and the complete public notice is available on the Bank of IP Success Stories page ([link para a página oficial](https://www.gov.br/inpi/pt-br/central-de-conteudo/casosdesucessoempi/banco-de-casos-de-sucesso-em-pi)), along with the PDF of the public notice ([link direto para o PDF do Edital nº 01/2026](https://www.gov.br/inpi/pt-br/central-de-conteudo/casosdesucessoempi/arquivos/sei_1497216_edital_1.pdf)).

## Who can participate

Eligible participants include:

-   **Companies** of any size
-   **Startups**
-   **Scientific, Technological, and Innovation Institutions (STIs)**
-   **Universities**
-   **Cooperatives and associations**
-   **Independent inventors**
-   **Individuals** who are Brazilian citizens or residents in Brazil

Legal entities must be constituted in accordance with Brazilian law, with headquarters and administration in the Country.

## Accepted IP Categories

The public notice accepts cases related to:

-   Trademarks
-   Patents
-   Industrial designs
-   Geographical indications
-   Computer programs
-   Topographies of integrated circuits

Participants must present experiences that demonstrate **concrete results** derived from the strategic use of these assets — such as economic value generation, institutional strengthening, innovation, territorial development, productive inclusion, or knowledge dissemination.

## Schedule

| Stage | Date |
|-------|------|
| Registration period | **June 23 to July 31, 2026** |
| Disclosure of preliminary results | **October 6, 2026** |
| Deadline for appeals | **October 7 to 14, 2026** |
| Publication of final results | **November 3, 2026** |

Registrations are made via an **electronic form** available on the Bank of Intellectual Property Success Stories page. In addition to the form, participants must submit the documentation specified in the public notice, including the **Descriptive Memorial of the Success Story** and supporting documents related to the initiative.

## Evaluation criteria

The eligible cases will be evaluated by a **Selection Committee** designated by the INPI Presidency, based on the following criteria:

-   **Strategic use of intellectual property**
-   **Economic impact**
-   **Social or territorial impact**
-   **Innovation**
-   **Educational and dissemination potential**

To be included in the Bank of Intellectual Property Success Stories, cases must achieve a **minimum score of 60 points**. The scoring is exclusively for evaluation purposes — it does not generate classification, ranking, or awards among participants.

## What it means to join the Bank

It is important to note that the **inclusion of a case in the Bank does not guarantee its dissemination by INPI**. Selected cases may be used by the Institute in institutional communication, education, campaigns, events, publications, and educational materials, according to institutional interest and subject to authorizations granted by participants.

Participation in the call **does not alter the ownership of intellectual property rights**, which remain with their respective owners. There is also no remuneration, prize, certification, or institutional seal granted.

## Why this matters

The Bank of Intellectual Property Success Stories is an initiative that recognizes the strategic role of IP in Brazil's development. By bringing together concrete experiences in the use of trademarks, patents, industrial designs, geographical indications, software, and topographies of integrated circuits, INPI creates a **repository of references** that can:

1.  **Inspire new inventors and entrepreneurs** to protect and value their creations
2.  **Demonstrate the return on investment** in IP for companies and institutions
3.  **Support public policies** for innovation and intellectual property
4.  **Strengthen the Brazilian innovation ecosystem** with real and verifiable cases

For companies that have built competitive advantages based on IP, this is an opportunity to record and disseminate this trajectory — contributing to Brazil's progress in the culture of protecting and valuing intangible assets.
