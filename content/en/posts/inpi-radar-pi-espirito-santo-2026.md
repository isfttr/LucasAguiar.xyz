The National Institute of Industrial Property (INPI) published, on July 3, 2026, the **51st Edition of the Technological Radar**, dedicated to mapping the use of the industrial property system by applicants from Espírito Santo. Developed in partnership with the Federal Institute of Education, Science and Technology of Espírito Santo (Ifes), the study covers the period from 2010 to November 2025 and analyzes patent applications, software registrations, trademarks, industrial designs, and geographical indications.

The data reveals a maturing innovation ecosystem, with universities as the main applicants, a strong presence of micro and small businesses, and a diversity of IP assets ranging from software to geographical indications for products such as clay pots and specialty coffees.

## The landscape of Espírito Santo patents

**519 published patent applications** were identified in the analyzed period, with the following distribution:

| Type | Quantity | % |
|------|-----------|--|
| Invention patents | 337 | 65% |
| Utility models | 177 | 34% |
| Certificates of addition | 5 | 1% |

About **70% of applications are still awaiting decision** — which is expected for recent filings —, while only **8% resulted in in-force patents**. This data highlights a known bottleneck in the system: the average examination time at INPI, which, although significantly improved in recent years, still impacts the conversion of applications into granted rights.

### Who applies?

The profile of applicants reveals a **significant participation of individuals**, either alone or in co-ownership with legal entities. Among legal entities, **micro and small businesses represent just over 31%** of the applications.

The main applicants by volume are **universities and research institutions**, notably:

- **Federal University of Espírito Santo (Ufes)**
- **Federal Institute of Espírito Santo (Ifes)**

The study also identified partnerships outside the state — with institutions from Rio de Janeiro, Minas Gerais, São Paulo, and Santa Catarina — and international partnerships, with universities from **Spain and the United States**.

### Technological fields

Mechanical Engineering (184 applications) and Chemistry (132 applications) lead in volume. The disaggregation into 35 technological sectors reveals areas of greater concentration such as **Civil Engineering, Special Machines, Medical Technology, Transport, and Furniture**.

## Software: 707 registrations on the rise

**707 software programs** were registered during the period, with a **notable growth trend from 2020**, associated with the acceleration of digitalization driven by the COVID-19 pandemic and the advancement of technologies such as artificial intelligence, cloud computing, and the Internet of Things (IoT).

The most frequent types of registered programs:

1.  **Applications** — 300 registrations
2.  **Information management** — 183 registrations

Ifes and Ufes once again stand out as the main applicants, and most registrations are in force.

## Trademarks: 68,492 applications in continuous growth

Trademark registration is, by far, the most used IP asset by Espírito Santo applicants, with **68,492 applications** in the period and a continuous growth trend.

| Class | Description | Applications |
|--------|-----------|---------|
| 35 | Business management, advertising, and marketing services | 17,257 |
| 41 | Education and entertainment | 7,753 |
| 25 | Apparel (main product class) | 4,189 |

Regarding the procedural status: **38.3% of trademarks are in force**, 18.6% were rejected, and 16.6% were archived.

## Industrial designs: 697 registrations

**697 industrial design registration applications** were filed and published. The main fields of application, according to the Locarno Classification:

-   **Furniture** (Class 06) — 143 registrations
-   **Packages and containers** (Class 09) — 112 registrations
-   **Building units and elements** (Class 25) — 91 registrations

Only **25% of registrations are in force**, suggesting that many applications may have been abandoned or not renewed.

## Geographical Indications: 11 recognized GIs

As of March 2026, Espírito Santo had **eleven geographical indications** recognized by INPI — nine indications of provenance and two appellations of origin. The products include:

-   Clay pots
-   Marble
-   Cocoa
-   Yam
-   Socol (typical sausage)
-   Coffees (in different varieties and processing)
-   Black pepper
-   Pink pepper
-   Beiju

The diversity of GIs — ranging from handicrafts to food — shows how industrial property can protect and valorize products with a territorial link, an increasingly relevant instrument for regional development.

## What the study reveals about Espírito Santo's innovation ecosystem

INPI's Technological Radar on Espírito Santo offers three important insights:

### 1. Universities as the engine of innovation

Ufes and Ifes lead patent and software applications. This is consistent with the national pattern, where public universities account for a large part of scientific and technological production. The challenge, however, is **technology transfer**: transforming patent applications into market innovations.

### 2. Micro and small businesses as protagonists

With 31% of patent applications, MPEs show that Espírito Santo has an **entrepreneurial fabric that sees value in IP**. Incentive and training programs can further expand this participation.

### 3. Trademarks lead, software grows

The expressive volume of trademark applications (68 thousand) contrasts with the still modest number of patents (519). This is expected: trademarks are more accessible and have a faster process. The growth in software registrations signals the **digitalization of the Espírito Santo economy**.

## How ES compares to the national scenario

To put the numbers in perspective: Brazil filed about **27 thousand patent applications** in 2025 (via PCT and direct filing). The 519 applications from ES represent approximately **1.9% of the national total** — a reasonable number for a state that accounts for about 2% of Brazil's GDP.

However, when we look at the [global innovation map]({{< relref "posts/mapa-global-patentes-pct-2025-brasil/" >}}), it is clear that Brazil as a whole still patents very little in relation to its economic potential. Regional studies like this are fundamental for diagnosing bottlenecks and guiding public innovation policies.
