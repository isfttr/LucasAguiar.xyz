The INPI published on June 29, 2026, the **Electronic Accreditation Notice No. 90.001/2026**, opening doors for professionals from all over Brazil to provide prior art search and state-of-the-art definition services for patent applications. It is the first time the Institute has outsourced this critical stage of patent examination in a structured way.

The model is simple: remote work, without exclusive dedication, payment per delivery. Each completed search is worth **R$ 1,025.62**. For those with technical training and knowledge in industrial property, it is a concrete opportunity to act as a public service provider without leaving home.

<!--more-->

## What is a Prior Art Search and Why Does it Matter

Before granting a patent, INPI needs to verify if the application meets the requirements of novelty, inventive step, and industrial applicability. The **prior art search** is the collection of existing technical documents and patents that may oppose the application — it is the factual basis on which the examiner makes their decision.

Historically, INPI performed these searches internally (dispatch 6.22) or utilized searches already conducted by foreign offices like USPTO and EPO for applications with foreign counterparts (dispatch 6.21). This model, however, has limitations: the patent backlog in Brazil is still significant, and the installed capacity of INPI's technical staff does not grow at the same speed as demand.

The new accreditation represents a structural change: instead of increasing the number of examiners, the Institute opted to **externalize the search stage**, maintaining the final decision on the patent with its internal examiners.

## Who Can Participate — Complete Requirements

The notice establishes clear criteria for qualification. The candidate must:

1. **Reside in Brazil**
2. Have a **complete higher education degree** in Engineering, Chemistry, Pharmacy, Biotechnology, Information Technology, or related fields as specified in the notice
3. Have **technical reading proficiency in English** — essential for analyzing patent documents and international articles
4. Possess an **active registration** in SICAF (Unified Supplier Registration System) and on the Federal Government's Procurement portal (Compras do Governo Federal)

In addition, it is mandatory to prove the completion of **three specific courses from the INPI Academy**:

- Prior art search for patent application examinations
- Use of the Derwent Patent Search tool
- Completion of the external search results form

And meet **one of two alternative requirements**:

- Have completed courses DL-101 and DL-318 from the World Intellectual Property Organization (WIPO); **or**
- Prove regular experience in the activity over the last three years

| Requirement | Detail |
|-----------|-------------|
| Education | Engineering, Chemistry, Pharmacy, Biotechnology, IT or related fields |
| Language | Technical reading in English |
| Registrations | Active SICAF + ComprasGov |
| INPI Courses | Prior art search, Derwent, Search form |
| Experience | WIPO courses (DL-101 + DL-318) OR 3 years of experience |

## Working Conditions and Remuneration

The accreditation does not create an employment relationship. The professional acts as an **accredited individual**, without exclusivity, and can even maintain other professional activities simultaneously.

- **Remuneration:** R$ 1,025.62 per completed search
- **Work format:** Remote
- **Validity of the notice:** 10 months (June 29, 2026 to April 29, 2027)
- **Evaluation cycles:** First cutoff date on July 29, 2026 (results by August 12, 2026); new stages in January and May 2027
- **Applications:** Continuous, via the official e-Pesquisa form

The value of R$ 1,025.62 per search is competitive when compared to outsourced search fees in the private market, especially considering that the professional does not need to bear client acquisition costs or office structure.

## Impact on the Brazilian Patent System

This movement by INPI needs to be read in the broader context of reforms to the Brazilian industrial property system.

The outsourcing of prior art searches can:

1. **Accelerate patent examination** — by shifting the most research-intensive stage to a scalable external workforce
2. **Reduce the backlog** — especially in areas with high volume of applications, such as pharmaceutical chemistry and mechanical engineering
3. **Create a specialized job market** — professionals with technical training can build a career in patent search without needing to relocate to Brasília
4. **Maintain decision quality** — the INPI examiner remains responsible for the merits; the accredited professional only provides the technical input

On the other hand, there are challenges. The training of accredited professionals needs to be homogeneous, and the Derwent tool requires specific proficiency. INPI will also need quality control mechanisms to ensure that external searches meet the required standard.

## How to Apply — Step by Step

1. Verify that you meet the education and language requirements
2. Complete the INPI Academy courses (available on the INPI website)
3. Complete WIPO's DL-101 and DL-318 courses (free, in Portuguese)
4. Register with SICAF and ComprasGov
5. Access the official e-Pesquisa form and submit your proposal
6. Await the evaluation cycle result

Questions can be directed to: busca_terceirizada@inpi.gov.br

## What to Observe in the Coming Months

The first batch of accredited professionals is expected to be announced by August 12, 2026. Until then, it will be interesting to monitor:

- How many professionals get accredited in the first stage — an indicator of the program's attractiveness
- The reaction of associations like ABPI and CNPL to the model
- Whether the program expands to other areas or becomes permanent after its 10-month validity period

If the pilot is successful, INPI may be paving the way for a hybrid patent examination model, combining internal capacity with an external network of specialists — something offices like the USPTO and EPO have been practicing for years.
