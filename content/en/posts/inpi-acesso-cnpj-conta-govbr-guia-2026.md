On July 6, 2026, the INPI published a clarification aimed at users who have doubts about the possibility of using the company's CNPJ to access Gov.br services — and, by extension, the Institute's electronic systems.

The question is recurring: Industrial Property professionals acting on behalf of companies need to deal with systems such as e-Marcas, e-Patentes, e-DI and e-Software. Many of these services require authentication via a gov.br account, and the question that arises is: **"Can I use the company's CNPJ instead of my personal account?"**

The INPI's answer is yes — but the path is not obvious.

## What the INPI clarified

The Institute reported that Gov.br offers a functionality that allows the person responsible for the company to link the CNPJ to their personal account and, from there, manage the employees authorized to act on behalf of the organization.

The notice was published on the INPI news page and directs users to the official federal government tutorial on [how to link CNPJ](https://www.gov.br/governodigital/pt-br/acessibilidade-e-usuario/atendimento-gov.br/duvidas-na-conta-gov.br/duvidas-na-vinculacao-de-cnpj-no-gov.br/como-vincular-cnpj).

In practice, this solves a common problem: IP offices, legal departments and independent inventors acting as legal entities need multiple employees to access INPI systems on behalf of the same company. Without the correct linking, each professional would have to use their personal account, making tracking and access management difficult.

## Step by step: how to link CNPJ to Gov.br

The process requires a digital certificate of the e-CNPJ type (A1 or A3) registered under the CPF of the company's legal representative. See the step-by-step:

1. **Access** [sso.acesso.gov.br](https://sso.acesso.gov.br/) and select the option **"Your digital certificate"**.
2. **Select the certificate** from the list (if more than one is installed) and enter the password.
3. After login, the gov.br account of the CPF holder will be displayed. Select **"Link Companies via e-CNPJ"**.
   - ⚠️ If this option does not appear, the certificate used is not a legal entity certificate. Contact the provider.
4. In the companies module, click **"Link company from e-CNPJ"**.
5. Check the displayed data (gov.br account of the holder + CNPJ to be linked) and click **"Link"**.
6. Done. The CNPJ is now linked to your gov.br account.

From then on, the person responsible for the company can:
- **Manage employees** — add or remove people authorized to act on behalf of the CNPJ
- **Import employees** in bulk
- **Deactivate the company** from the account, if necessary

> ⚠️ The relationship is checked in the Federal Revenue system. If the message "CPF has no relationship with CNPJ" appears, it means the individual has no formal link with the company in the RFB registry.

## Why this matters for INPI users

The digitalization of INPI services has advanced rapidly. In 2026, the Institute already requires that most applications be made exclusively through electronic systems — such as the [new Patent Services Module]({{< relref "posts/inpi-modulo-servicos-patentes-novas-funcionalidades-2026/" >}}), which replaced Electronic Petitioning for patent filings.

Having the CNPJ correctly linked to Gov.br means that:

| Scenario | Without linking | With linking |
|---------|---------------|----------------|
| Access to INPI systems | Personal account only | Personal account or on behalf of the company |
| Team management | Each professional with individual access | Admin centralizes and manages employees |
| Tracking | Hard to associate actions with the company | Actions linked to the CNPJ |
| Electronic power of attorney | Physical power of attorney required | Digital management of powers of attorney |

## How to access INPI services after linking

Once the CNPJ is linked to the gov.br account, access to INPI systems follows the normal flow:

1. Access the [INPI services portal](https://www.gov.br/inpi/pt-br/servicos)
2. Choose the desired system (e-Marcas, e-Patentes, e-DI, e-Software, e-IG, e-Chip, e-Contratos)
3. Log in with your gov.br account — the system will automatically recognize the link to the CNPJ
4. If there are multiple linked CNPJs, select the one you want to use

A particularly useful feature for IP offices is the ability to maintain a **centralized registry of employees** authorized to file applications on behalf of the company. This avoids the need for physical powers of attorney for each professional and streamlines day-to-day operations.

## What changes in practice

The INPI publication is a timely reminder. Many users still face difficulties with authentication in the Institute's systems — whether due to unfamiliarity with the CNPJ linking feature or issues with digital certificates.

The official clarification does not bring regulatory changes, but it plays an important role in **guiding users**, especially at a time when the INPI has been modernizing its digital infrastructure. Those who have already experienced a stuck filing due to access problems know how much time and rework it costs.

For those who have not yet linked their CNPJ, this is one of those simple steps that avoids future headaches — especially with the tight schedule of deadlines at the INPI.
