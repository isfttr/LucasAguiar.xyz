Every election year brings a round of adjustments in the institutional communication of public bodies — and the National Institute of Industrial Property (INPI) is no exception.

As of **July 4, 2026**, the INPI has adopted temporary measures to adapt its communication channels in compliance with Law No. 9,504/1997 (Elections Law) and the guidelines of the Attorney General's Office (AGU) and the Presidency's Social Communication Secretariat (Secom/PR).

If you follow INPI news, participate in public consultations, or need the Institute's services during the election period, this guide covers everything you need to know.

## What changes in communication channels

The restrictions, provided for in art. 73, item VI, letter "b" of the Elections Law, limit institutional advertising by public bodies during the election period. In practice, the following changes have been in effect since July 4:

| Channel | Change | Period |
|---------|--------|--------|
| **News Portal** (gov.br/inpi) | News published until July 3 have been archived; only public utility and service delivery content will be published | Jul 4 — Oct 25 |
| **Instagram** (@inpi_br) | Current profile deactivated; communication via temporary profile | Jul 4 — Oct 25 |
| **Facebook** (Inpi_br) | Current profile deactivated; communication via temporary profile | Jul 4 — Oct 25 |
| **LinkedIn** (INPI_br) | Current profile deactivated; communication via temporary profile | Jul 4 — Oct 25 |
| **YouTube** | Channel remains at usual address, but previously published videos become unavailable | Jul 4 — Oct 25 |
| **INPI Portal** (services) | **Nothing changes** — essential services, systems, and institutional information continue normally | Normal |

During the restriction period, the INPI started using specific temporary profiles on social media:

## What does NOT change: essential services continue

The most important message of this guide: **INPI services continue to operate normally throughout the entire election period.**

This means you can, without any changes:

- **File patent, trademark, industrial design, and GI applications** normally via e-INPI
- **Track the progress of proceedings** — see our complete guide on [how to check the status of a proceeding at INPI]({{< relref "posts/como-consultar-andamento-processo-inpi/" >}})
- **Pay fees and GRUs** through normal channels
- **Participate in public consultations and hearings** — notifications continue to be published in the RPI and on the Portal
- **Access the Industrial Property Journal (RPI)** — weekly editions continue to be published
- **Request prior art search services** — including the [accreditation program for patent searches]({{< relref "posts/inpi-credenciamento-busca-anterioridade-patentes/" >}})
- **Register trademarks** — all procedures remain unchanged

The INPI Portal (**gov.br/inpi**) remains the main relationship channel with users during the electoral closed season, with all institutional information and services available.

## Why this matters for those who live on IP

For industrial property professionals — Industrial Property agents, lawyers, companies with an active IP portfolio — service continuity is what really matters. The restrictions affect only institutional communication and advertising, not the core systems.

Some practical implications:

1. **Public consultations and important announcements** will continue to be published, but mainly via the Portal and RPI — not just on social media. Those who rely exclusively on LinkedIn or Instagram to get information about INPI will need to pay extra attention to the Portal.

2. **Public notices and calls** continue to be published in the RPI (Section I), which remains available. The RPI is INPI's official communication vehicle — not social media.

3. **The archiving of previous news** means that information published before July 4 will no longer be visible in the public news area. If you need to check any news published in the first half of 2026, it's worth saving the links or searching via archiving tools.

## Comparison with previous elections

This is not the first time INPI has adopted election adaptation measures. The cycle repeats every federal election, following the same legal provision (art. 73, VI, "b" of Law 9,504/1997). The difference in 2026 is the scope of restrictions on social media — a reflection of the growing importance of these channels in institutional communication.

In the 2022 elections, INPI also restricted its communication, but the social media ecosystem was less central to providing information to users. In 2026, with the consolidation of LinkedIn as an active communication channel between INPI and the IP community, the temporary deactivation — even with substitute profiles — requires adaptation.

## Practical recommendations

If you rely on INPI for your daily IP work, some simple actions can help you get through the period without surprises:

- ✅ **Access the Portal regularly:** `gov.br/inpi` is the official and most complete channel during the closed season
- ✅ **Follow the RPI weekly:** Section I (Notices) continues to be the official vehicle for announcements and public notices
- ✅ **Save important links:** news and announcements from 2026 before July 4 may no longer be accessible — make backups
- ✅ **Use e-INPI normally:** electronic petitioning systems are not affected
- ✅ **Follow the temporary profiles:** if you use social media to follow INPI, migrate to the temporary channels indicated above
