A Geographical Indication (GI) identifies a product or service whose qualities, reputation, or characteristics are essentially due to its geographical origin. In Brazil, GIs are regulated by the Industrial Property Law (Law No. 9.279/96) and registered with the INPI. This guide covers all practical aspects: the types of GIs, who can apply, required documents, costs, and the step-by-step process.

> In July 2026, the INPI opened a **public call for submissions** for the recognition of Argentine Geographical Indications in Brazil (deadline until 07/31/2026), demonstrating the growing relevance of this protection mechanism also in international relations. Visit the [official consultation page](https://www.gov.br/inpi/pt-br/central-de-conteudo/noticias/tomada-publica-de-subsidios-sobre-indicacoes-geograficas-argentinas-esta-aberta) to participate.

## What is a Geographical Indication?

A GI is an institute of industrial property that protects the link between a product and its territory of origin. Unlike patents (which protect inventions) or trademarks (which identify the business origin of products), the GI protects the **reputation associated with a geographical location**.

The legal basis is found in **Articles 176 to 182 of Law No. 9.279/96 (IPL)**, which define two modalities:

| Aspect | Indication of Source (IP) | Denomination of Origin (DO) |
|---------|-------------------------------|----------------------------|
| **Concept** | Geographical name known as a production center for a given product or service | Geographical name whose qualities are exclusively or essentially due to the geographical environment (natural and human factors) |
| **Requirement** | Only reputation of the place | Intrinsic quality linked to the territory |
| **Degree of proof** | Simpler — just demonstrate that the place is recognized as a production center | More complex — requires technical reports, soil/climate studies, geographical delimitation |
| **Brazilian examples** | Cachaça from Minas Gerais, Green Propolis from Paraná | Vales da Uva Goethe (SC), Coffee from the Cerrado Mineiro Region (MG) |

The IPL defines in Art. 176 the indication of source, in Art. 177 the denomination of origin, and in Art. 178 the impediments to registration. Art. 182 establishes that, in addition to legal protection, the INPI may establish complementary conditions — which is done through **INPI/PR Ordinance No. 04/2022** (amended by Ordinance No. 051/2024 and Ordinance No. 50/2026).

## Who can apply for registration

The following may apply for GI registration at the INPI:

- **Unions, associations, institutes** and any legal entity representing the community authorized as a procedural substitute
- Registration is **collective** — it does not belong to an individual producer, but rather to a representative entity that administers it
- Producers or service providers established in the respective territory may use the GI, provided they meet the requirements defined in the rules of use

## Step-by-step registration at the INPI

### 1. Prepare the documentation

Documents vary according to the type of GI (IP or DO) and the nature of the applicant. In general terms, the following are required:

**For both types:**
- Registration application (electronic form in the e-IG system)
- Proof of payment of the GRU
- Bylaws of the applicant entity (with the purpose of representing the community)
- Rules of use (document defining who may use the GI and the conditions)
- Instrument proving the applicant's legitimacy

**Specific for Indication of Source (IP):**
- Documents proving that the geographical name became known as a production center for the product or service provision (historical materials, news reports, publications, fair certificates, etc.)

**Specific for Denomination of Origin (DO):**
- Delimitation of the geographical area
- Description of the qualities or characteristics of the product or service
- Technical and scientific reports proving the influence of the geographical environment (natural factors such as soil, climate, altitude; and human factors such as traditional production techniques)
- Description of the process for obtaining the product

> Refer to chapter 7 of the [INPI Manual of Geographical Indications](https://www.gov.br/inpi/pt-br/servicos/indicacoes-geograficas/guia-basico) (in effect since 02/01/2021) for the detailed list. Also check the [Technical Specifications Notebooks](https://www.gov.br/inpi/pt-br/servicos/indicacoes-geograficas/fichas-tecnicas-de-indicacoes-geograficas) of already registered GIs as a reference.

### 2. Pay the GRU

It is necessary to issue the Federal Union Collection Guide (GRU) in the INPI system. The amounts follow the **Fee Table** (INPI/PR Ordinance No. 10/2025, in effect):

| Service | Amount (R$) |
|---------|-----------|
| Application for registration of a Geographical Indication | **R$ 3,200.00** |
| Application for registration of GI (ME, EPP, MEI, non-profit entities) | **R$ 1,600.00** |
| Appeal against rejection | **R$ 700.00** |
| Administrative nullity | **R$ 800.00** |
| Appeal petition (second instance) | According to current table |

> Check the [official cost table](https://www.gov.br/inpi/pt-br/servicos/indicacoes-geograficas/custos) for updated amounts and the [GRU issuance system](https://www.gov.br/inpi/pt-br/servicos/indicacoes-geograficas/custos) for payment.

**Attention:** GRU due dates are independent of procedural deadlines. Do not confuse the payment due date with the legal deadline for submitting documents or appeals.

### 3. Initiate the application in the e-IG system

Since **INPI PR Resolution No. 233/2019**, all GI applications are **exclusively electronic** — it is no longer possible to file on paper. The system used is **e-IG**, accessible through the INPI portal.

Steps:
1. Register at [e-INPI](https://www.gov.br/inpi/pt-br/servicos/indicacoes-geograficas/e-ig) (if you do not already have an account)
2. Access the e-IG system with your account
3. Fill in the electronic form with the applicant and GI data
4. Attach the mandatory documentation
5. Enter the number of the paid GRU
6. Submit the application

The complete electronic filing guide is attached to the GI Manual.

### 4. Follow up on the process

After filing, the INPI conducts the **formal analysis** (documentation verification) and, if approved, the **merit analysis** (verification of legal requirements for grant). During the process, corrections or additional documents may be required.

Main steps:

| Step | Indicative deadline | Description |
|-------|-----------------|-----------|
| Filing | Day 0 | Submission in e-IG with paid GRU |
| Formal analysis | 30-60 days | Verification of documents and regularity |
| Publication in the RPI | After formal analysis | Opening for third-party submissions |
| Merit analysis | 6-18 months | Examination of requirements (IP or DO) |
| Grant | — | Publication of approval in the RPI |
| Certificate | After grant | Available for download on the portal |

Follow the progress via the **Industrial Property Journal (RPI)**, published on Tuesdays, and the [process search database](https://www.gov.br/inpi/pt-br/servicos/indicacoes-geograficas/busca).

### 5. After grant

Once the registration is granted, the **Registration Certificate** is made available in electronic format on the INPI portal for download. The GI has **indefinite** protection, as long as the conditions that gave rise to it are maintained — unlike patents (20 years) or trademarks (10 years, renewable).

The holder must ensure compliance with the rules of use and may adopt measures to combat counterfeiting. The INPI maintains a [National Directory for Combating Counterfeiting of Geographical Indications (DNCF-IGs)](https://www.gov.br/inpi/pt-br/projetos-estrategicos/combate-a-falsificacao/dncf-igs) as a support tool.

## Why register a GI?

The registration of a Geographical Indication brings concrete benefits to producers and regions:

- **Market differentiation** — GI products compete on quality and reputation, not just price
- **Value addition** — studies show that GI products can have a 20% to 50% premium over similar non-certified products
- **Regional development** — the GI enhances local identity, attracts tourism, and stimulates the regional economy
- **Legal protection** — prevents unauthorized use of the geographical name by third parties
- **Market access** — GIs are internationally recognized by the TRIPS/WTO Agreement, facilitating exports

## Differences between GIs and other IP institutes

| Characteristic | Geographical Indication | Collective Trademark | Certification Trademark |
|----------------|----------------------|----------------|----------------------|
| **Object** | Geographical origin + reputation/quality | Identification of members of an entity | Compliance with technical standards |
| **Holder** | Representative entity | Association/union/cooperative | Independent certifying entity |
| **Use** | By producers in the geographical area | By members of the entity | By those who obtain certification |
| **Term of protection** | Indefinite | 10 years (renewable) | 10 years (renewable) |
| **Legal basis** | Arts. 176-182 of the IPL | Art. 123 of the IPL | Art. 123 of the IPL |

## Complete legal framework

The legislation applicable to Geographical Indications in Brazil includes:

**National:**
- **Law No. 9.279/96 (IPL)** — Arts. 176 to 182 ([full text](https://www.planalto.gov.br/ccivil_03/leis/l9279.htm))
- **INPI/PR Ordinance No. 04/2022** — Conditions for GI registration (as amended by Ordinances No. 051/2024 and No. 50/2026)
- **INPI/PR Ordinance No. 50/2026** — Amends provisions on GI registration (January/2026)
- **INPI Manual of Geographical Indications** (in effect since 02/01/2021)

**International:**
- **TRIPS Agreement** (WTO) — Arts. 22 to 24 ([text on WIPO portal](https://www.wipo.int/treaties/en/agreement/trips.html))
- **Paris Convention for the Protection of Industrial Property** — Arts. 1(2) and 10 (promulgated by [Decree No. 75.572/1975](https://www.planalto.gov.br/ccivil_03/decreto/1970-1979/D75572.htm) and [Decree No. 635/1992](https://www.planalto.gov.br/ccivil_03/decreto/1990-1994/D0635.htm))

Check the [INPI legislation page](https://www.gov.br/inpi/pt-br/servicos/indicacoes-geograficas/legislacao-indicacao-geografica-1) for the complete list of regulations.

## Final considerations

The Geographical Indication is one of the most strategic instruments of industrial property for valuing regional products and promoting local development. Unlike patents and trademarks, its term of protection is indefinite — making it a permanent asset for producing regions that invest in quality and reputation.

The registration process at the INPI requires planning and technical documentation, especially in the case of Denomination of Origin, but the benefits in terms of added value, legal protection, and market access offset the investment.

For Brazilian producers, associations, and cooperatives interested in protecting their regional identity, the first step is to consult the INPI to verify the feasibility of registration and gather the necessary documentation.
