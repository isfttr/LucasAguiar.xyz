The INPI currently offers **17 modalities of priority processing for patents** — 15 aimed at the general public and 2 for public entities. On July 1, 2026, the resumption of accepting requests for symbol H04 (Electric Communication Technique) reignited interest from telecommunications technology applicants, who can now request prioritization again under new per-applicant quota rules.

This guide consolidates all current modalities, their costs, required documents, and the step-by-step process for requesting priority processing with the INPI.

## What is patent priority processing?

Since September 2020, the INPI replaced the former "priority examination" with the broader concept of **priority processing**. While the old model was limited to the queue of applications awaiting technical examination, priority processing covers **all activities of the patent process** — from filing documentation to the end of processing (archiving, rejection, or expiration).

This means that if an elderly applicant is entitled to priority processing, that prioritization also applies to a title transfer or any other procedural act, even after the grant of the patent.

The main regulation is [INPI/PR Ordinance No. 79/2022](https://www.gov.br/inpi/pt-br/assuntos/legislacao/portarias/2022/portaria-inpi-pr-no-79-de-22-de-setembro-de-2022), which governs the priority processing of patent proceedings within the INPI.

## All 17 modalities of priority processing

The modalities are divided into four categories, according to the reason for the prioritization request.

### Due to the applicant

| Modality | Who can request | GRU Code | Amount | Discount for ME/EPP/ICT |
|----------|----------------|----------|--------|--------------------------|
| Elderly applicant (60+) | Natural person ≥ 60 years | 263 | Exempt | — |
| Applicant with disability | Natural person with physical or mental disability | 263 | Exempt | — |
| Applicant with serious illness | Natural person with a serious illness | 263 | Exempt | — |
| Microentrepreneur, ME or EPP | MEI, ME or EPP | 279 | R$ 890 | R$ 445 |
| ICT | Scientific, Technological and Innovation Institution | 279 | R$ 890 | R$ 445 |
| Startup | Startup (per Supplementary Law 182/2021) | 279 | R$ 890 | R$ 445 |

**Required documents:**
- Elderly: official identification document showing age
- Disability: medical report from a public administration physician
- Serious illness: medical report from a public administration physician
- ME/EPP/ICT/Startup: certificate issued by a public authority indicating the classification

### Due to the situation

| Modality | Who can request | GRU Code | Amount |
|----------|----------------|----------|--------|
| Grant condition to obtain financial resources | Any applicant | 279 | R$ 890 |
| Reproduced by third parties (counterfeiting) | Any applicant | 279 | R$ 890 |
| Third party accused of counterfeiting | Any accused third party | 279 | R$ 890 |
| Prior user of the technology | Any holder of prior use | 279 | R$ 890 |
| Technology resulting from public funding | Applicant or supporting entity | 279 | R$ 890 |
| Technology available on the market | Applicant or third parties | 279 | R$ 890 |

**Required documents:**
- Release of financial resources: contract from the financial institution linking the release
- Counterfeiting (applicant): evidence of counterfeiting + notification + receipt
- Counterfeiting (third party): proof of being accused + clarification
- Prior user: clarification + evidence
- Public funding: release instrument + declaration
- Market: evidence + declaration

### Due to the technology

| Modality | Who can request | GRU Code | Amount |
|----------|----------------|----------|--------|
| Green technology | Any applicant | 279 | R$ 890 |
| Health treatment (specific diseases) | Applicant, third parties or public authorities | 279 | R$ 890 |
| Technology requested by the Ministry of Health | Ministry of Health | None | Exempt |
| Public interest or national emergency | Act of the Federal Executive Branch | Ex officio | Exempt |

**Required documents:**
- Green technology / Health treatment: clarifications about the subject matter
- MoH / Public interest: official government act

### Due to cooperation (PPH)

| Modality | Who can request | GRU Code | Amount |
|----------|----------------|----------|--------|
| Patent family initiated in Brazil | Any applicant | 279 | R$ 890 |
| Patentable subject matter by partner office (PPH) | Any applicant | 277 | R$ 1,780 |

**Required documents:**
- Family Brazil: proof of initiation of the patent family in Brazil
- PPH: examination result from the partner office + other documents

> 💡 **PPH (Patent Prosecution Highway):** allows patent applications already considered patentable by partner offices (USPTO, EPO, JPO, etc.) to receive priority examination at the INPI. The program has a global limit of **3,200 requests per year**.

## News: Resumption of H04 priority processing (July 2026)

On June 30, 2026, [INPI/PR/DIRPA Ordinance No. 5, of June 23, 2026](https://www.gov.br/inpi/pt-br/central-de-conteudo/noticias/retoramado-tramite-prioritario-de-patentes-de-tecnica-de-comunicacao-eletrica-h04) was published in RPI 2895, resuming the acceptance of priority processing requests for patent applications classified under symbol **H04 (Electric Communication Technique)** — the classification covering telecommunications, networks, data transmission, and wireless communication systems.

**Specific H04 rules:**

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Per-applicant quota | 1 request/month (2 in July 2026, exceptionally) |
| Scope | All modalities of priority processing, including PPH |
| Exclusions | Elderly, disabled, seriously ill, and startup (quota does not apply) |
| PPH Limit | 3,200/year (maintained, regardless of H04) |
| July 2026 exception | PPH accepted even with office action 6.23 (Preliminary Requirement) |

For telecommunications patent applicants, this resumption represents a significant opportunity to speed up grant, especially in a sector where the technology cycle is short and patent protection needs to keep pace with innovation.

## Step-by-step to request

### 1. Identify the applicable modality

Use the [INPI questionnaire](https://www.gov.br/inpi/pt-br/servicos/patentes/tramite-prioritario) to determine which modality applies to your case. The main variables are: applicant profile, situation of the process, and technology involved.

### 2. Gather the supporting documentation

Each modality requires specific documents (see tables above). Supporting documents are essential — requests without the correct documentation are rejected.

### 3. Generate the GRU

The Federal Union Collection Guide (GRU) must be issued in the INPI system with the corresponding service code:
- **Code 263** — Priority examination (exemptions: elderly, disabled, seriously ill)
- **Code 279** — Strategic priority examination (R$ 890 / R$ 445 with discount)
- **Code 277** — Collaborative priority examination / PPH (R$ 1,780 / R$ 890 with discount)

### 4. File the request via e-Patentes or GRU

The request is made through the INPI electronic system (e-Patentes or GRU, depending on the code), attaching the supporting documentation and the paid GRU.

### 5. Track the progress

Check the status via the [INPI procedural monitoring system]({{< relref "posts/como-consultar-andamento-processo-inpi/" >}}). Priority processing significantly reduces waiting time but does not eliminate the need for active monitoring.

## Frequently asked questions

### Does priority processing guarantee patent grant?

No. Priority processing speeds up the procedure, but the substantive examination follows the same technical and legal criteria. Prioritization does not affect patent rights — the patent will remain in force until its expiration regardless of the outcome of the prioritization request.

### Can I request priority processing at any stage of the process?

Yes. Unlike the former "priority examination," priority processing applies to all activities of the process, regardless of the stage — from filing to after grant.

### Are PPH and H04 priority processing cumulative?

Yes. PPH is a separate modality within priority processing. For H04 applications, PPH is also subject to the limit of 1 request/month per applicant (except elderly, disabled, seriously ill, and startup), in addition to the global annual limit of 3,200 PPH requests.

### Can an individual microentrepreneur get a discount?

Yes. MEI, micro-enterprise (ME), and small business (EPP) are entitled to a 50% discount on the GRU amount (code 279), upon presentation of a classification certificate issued by a public authority.

## Why does priority processing exist?

In 2019, the INPI had approximately **150 thousand pending patent applications**. The [Backlog Reduction Plan](https://www.gov.br/inpi/pt-br/servicos/patentes/plano-de-combate-ao-backlog) tackled 80% of that stock, but priority processing serves a different need: allowing strategic innovations — green, health, startups, or publicly funded — to reach the market faster.

The system of 17 modalities seeks to balance two objectives: to expedite technologies of public interest and to offer accelerated paths for applicants who demonstrate a specific need. As we saw in the case of the quota exhaustion for trademarks in e-commerce, the limit of slots per period is real and can impact those who do not plan ahead.
