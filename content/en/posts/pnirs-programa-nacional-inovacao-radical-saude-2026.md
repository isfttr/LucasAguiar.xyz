The Ministry of Health published in the Official Gazette of the Union on July 6, 2026, **Ordinance GM/MS No. 11.921**, which establishes the **National Program for Radical Innovation in Health (PNIRS)** within the scope of the Ministry of Health.

The ordinance, signed by Minister **Alexandre Rocha Santos Padilha**, amends Consolidation Ordinance GM/MS No. 5 of September 28, 2017, to insert Annex CVIII, which establishes the complete structure of the new program.

## What is PNIRS

PNIRS is a program focused on the development of **radical innovations in health** — defined as technologies new to the country or to the international scene, characterized by high technological risk, high impact potential, and capacity to transform scientific, productive, healthcare, or regulatory competencies.

The program will be coordinated by the **Secretariat of Science, Technology, and Innovation in Health (SECTIS/MS)** and operationalized through **Anchor Centers for Radical Innovation in Health** — institutions with specialized scientific and technological infrastructure.

## Objectives

- Accelerate the transformation of scientific knowledge into products, processes, and services of interest to the SUS
- Expand the national capacity for technological development in health
- Support the development of technologies at different levels of technological maturity
- Broaden the productive sector's access to advanced scientific and technological infrastructures
- Promote interaction among academia, government, productive sector, health services, and regulatory bodies
- Strengthen technological autonomy and national health sovereignty

## Guidelines

The program follows guidelines such as:

- Focus on structural challenges of the SUS
- Strengthening the national scientific, technological, and productive base
- Encouraging translational research and emerging and disruptive technologies — including new molecules, advanced therapies, medical devices, biotechnologies, and digital health solutions
- Interministerial and intersectoral coordination
- Continuous monitoring and evaluation of results and impacts

## How it works

PNIRS operates through two complementary modalities:

1. **Provision of specialized technological services** to the productive sector, making available infrastructure, equipment, technological platforms, and specialized teams
2. **Execution of R&D&I projects** (Research, Development, and Innovation) aimed at developing strategic technologies for health

R&D&I projects will be selected on a **continuous flow** basis, with analysis of scientific merit, potential impact on the SUS, and technical, economic, and regulatory feasibility. The project portfolio will be structured in phases, with periodic monitoring and the possibility of continuation, redirection, or discontinuation.

## Governance

The governance structure of PNIRS consists of:

- **Executive Coordination** — formed by the Executive Secretariat of the MS, the Department of Science and Technology of SECTIS, and the Department of the Economic and Industrial Health Complex
- **Selection and Monitoring Committee** — responsible for technical evaluation and decision on project continuity
- **Advisory Council** — strategic advisory body
- **Anchor Centers for Radical Innovation in Health** — executing institutions

## Context

PNIRS was launched by **President Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva** at an event at **CNPEM** (National Center for Research in Energy and Materials), in Campinas (SP), which also marked the delivery of new beamlines of the **Sirius** accelerator. The first PNIRS hub was installed at CNPEM, with investments linked to the **New PAC** (Growth Acceleration Program).

The ordinance does not specify financial amounts — budget execution will be enabled through specific legal instruments entered into with the Anchor Centers, covering mechanisms such as technological procurement, partnerships for productive development (PDP), and other funding instruments.
