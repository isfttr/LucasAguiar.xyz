The INPI published on June 26, 2026, the latest update to the **Industrial Designs Manual**, the document that consolidates the rules and procedures for the registration of industrial designs in Brazil. Although the update is specific in scope — copies of documents, unionist priority, and petitions for compliance with requirements — it arrives at a strategic moment for the sector.

Brazil joined the **Hague Agreement** in 2023, allowing for unified international filing of industrial designs via WIPO. Since then, the volume of applications has grown, and INPI has been adjusting its internal procedures to deal with the new reality. This update to the Manual is another step in that direction.

## What changed in the Industrial Designs Manual

The June 26, 2026 update brought three main areas of change:

### 1. Request for document copies

The Manual now includes **clearer guidelines** on how users should request copies of documents related to industrial design processes. The change aims to standardize the procedure and reduce response time — a recurring demand from IP firms and applicants.

### 2. Proof of unionist priority right obtained by assignment

One of the most relevant changes from a legal point of view: the Manual now details how to prove the **unionist priority right** when it has been acquired through **assignment**. Unionist priority (Article 4 of the Paris Union Convention — CUP) allows an applicant to claim the date of the first filing made in a member country to file the same design in Brazil within a 6-month period.

When the priority right is transferred from one holder to another (for example, from the original inventor to the company that will commercialize the product), the documentary proof must be precise. The update clarifies exactly which documents are accepted and how they should be presented.

### 3. Update of attachments for petitions of compliance with requirements

INPI also reviewed the **information regarding the attachments** that must accompany petitions for compliance with requirements. Petitioners who fail to attach correct documentation have their applications denied — so this is an important practical point for those with ongoing processes.

## Why this matters now

The timing of the update is not random. Some contextual factors deserve attention:

| Factor | Impact |
|---|---|
| **Brazil in the Hague Agreement** | Since 2023, Brazil receives international industrial design filings via WIPO — the Manual needs to be aligned with the procedures of the Hague system |
| **Growth of the design market** | The registration of industrial designs has grown in Brazil in recent years, driven by sectors such as packaging, furniture, and electronics |
| **Integration with digital systems** | INPI has been modernizing its systems (INPI 4.0), and the Manual needs to reflect the new electronic workflows |
| **Riyadh Design Law Treaty** | WIPO recently launched the Riyadh Design Law Treaty, which may bring new international alignments — the first accession (Albania) occurred in June 2026 |

## What is an industrial design?

For those unfamiliar with the topic, **industrial design** protects the **ornamental appearance** of a product — its shape, lines, colors, textures, or materials. It is different from a patent (which protects the function/invention) and a trademark (which protects commercial identity).

Examples of registrable industrial designs:
- The shape of a bottle
- The design of packaging
- The visual configuration of a piece of furniture
- The layout of a user interface (in some cases)

Industrial design protection in Brazil lasts **10 years from the filing date**, renewable for up to three successive periods of 5 years (maximum total of 25 years).

## How to consult the updated Manual

The complete Industrial Designs Manual is available on the INPI page. The updates page records the history of changes and previous versions of the document, allowing users to follow the evolution of procedures.

> **Official Link:** [INPI — Desenhos Industriais](https://www.gov.br/inpi/pt-br/servicos/desenhos-industriais)

## Points of attention for applicants

1. **Unionist priority by assignment:** If you are filing an industrial design in Brazil based on priority obtained by assignment from another holder, review the documentation before filing — the new rules are more specific about what is accepted.

2. **Copies of documents:** The procedure has been standardized. Consult the Manual before requesting copies to avoid rework.

3. **Compliance with requirements:** Check the mandatory attachments before responding to an INPI requirement. Incomplete documentation can lead to the dismissal of the application.

## What to observe in the coming months

The industrial designs ecosystem in Brazil is undergoing transformation. With the Hague Agreement consolidated, the new Riyadh Treaty under discussion, and the modernization of INPI's systems, it is likely that we will see more updates to the Manual over the next few months.

Companies working with industrial design — especially in the packaging, furniture, electronics, and automotive sectors — should keep their IP teams attentive to changes.
